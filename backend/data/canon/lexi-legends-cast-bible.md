# Lexi Legends Cast Bible

Status: Phase 1-2 creative bible
Canonical sources: `ip-strategy-revised.md`; detailed age-specific character sheets under `cast/`
Created: 2026-05-19

## Purpose

This bible defines the recurring cast for Lexi Legends and gives writers, artists, product designers, and future prompt templates stable character anchors. The goal is variety without drift: each story can feature different genres, supporting characters, and conflicts while the recurring heroes remain recognizable.

For exact age-specific visual details, load the matching individual character sheet from `cast/`. This bible summarizes the current state; the individual sheets win when a precise outfit, tool, color, age proportion, pose rule, or "Do Not" constraint is needed.

## Cast Design Philosophy

The recurring heroes should feel like a premium animated ensemble. Each has:
- A clear silhouette.
- A hard-coded color palette.
- A unique writing tool.
- A distinct way of using Ink.
- A learner identity that kids can recognize without ranking the characters.

No hero is "the smart one." Each character is intelligent in a different way. Their differences should model that vocabulary learning can be creative, careful, physical, social, emotional, playful, or analytical.

Story-specific characters may appear freely when they support the premise. Realm-native characters can be witnesses, rivals, helpers, family members, classmates, inventors, shopkeepers, captains, performers, officials, neighbors, or any other age-appropriate role. They should enrich the specific story without displacing the recurring hero focus.

## Away Team Rules

Default cast per graphic novel:
- 1 or 2 recurring heroes.
- Folio as a flexible vocabulary guide.
- Any number of story-specific supporting characters needed for clarity, emotion, mystery, humor, or realm texture.

Use 1 hero + Folio when:
- The story benefits from emotional focus.
- The protagonist needs a clear personal arc.
- The genre is mystery, slice-of-life, solo challenge, first discovery, quiet exploration, or repair.
- Image clarity matters.
- The vocabulary pack maps strongly to one learner identity.

Use 2 heroes + Folio when:
- Contrast, banter, or teamwork improves the story.
- The vocabulary words naturally split into different approaches.
- The genre is action, heist, rescue, debate, competition, buddy adventure, or split problem-solving.
- One hero's strength should challenge or complement another hero's growth edge.

Normal pack stories should not use more than 2 recurring heroes unless explicitly treated as a special event. This keeps images readable and gives each hero room to matter.

Folio does not need to appear visibly in every panel. Folio can be physical, projected, heard, represented through notes, present mainly on the review page, or lightly implied.

## Recurring Heroes

### Leo

Role: The Creative Rookie

Learner identity: Creative, experimental, expressive. Leo tries ideas out loud and learns by making something visible.

Visual anchors:
- Bright crimson red oversized hoodie with rolled-up sleeves.
- Casual sneakers and street-art details.
- Youthful, energetic posture.
- Cyan Ink glow from his spray can.

Writing weapon:
- A glowing cyan spray paint can.
- Leo tags words in the air, across walls, on signs, and over broken spaces.
- His Ink style is bold, graphic, fast, and improvisational.

Personality:
- Curious, brave, funny, and sometimes impulsive.
- Good at seeing surprising connections.
- Willing to try first, even before he fully understands.

Growth edge:
- Leo sometimes reaches for style before precision.
- His best stories show him learning that a word has more power when it fits exactly.

Dialogue voice:
- Short, energetic lines.
- Uses playful metaphors and quick observations.
- Can admit confusion without shame.

Vocabulary learning model:
- Shows that experimentation is useful, but meaning matters.
- Good for stories where a rough first attempt becomes a precise final use.

Solo-story strengths:
- Rookie confidence arc.
- Creative problem-solving.
- Graffiti, performance, street-level mysteries, social courage, and "try again" stories.

Image prompt reference:
- Leo: creative rookie wearing a bright crimson oversized hoodie with rolled sleeves, casual sneakers, energetic posture, holding a glowing cyan spray paint can that makes magical graffiti words.

### Amara

Role: The Lore Scholar

Learner identity: Studious, thorough, curious about history and hidden structure. Amara learns by investigating.

Visual anchors:
- Warm medium-brown skin.
- Voluminous natural hair.
- Purple and gold starry cloak over modern street clothes.
- Golden Ink glow from her quill.

Writing weapon:
- A massive glowing golden quill.
- Amara writes elegant words, notes, diagrams, and cursive strokes.
- Her Ink style is precise, luminous, archival, and graceful.

Personality:
- Patient, observant, thoughtful, and quietly bold.
- Loves old maps, marginal notes, hidden records, and patterns.
- Can be dryly funny when others rush.

Growth edge:
- Amara can over-research or hesitate because she wants certainty.
- Her best stories show her trusting a well-supported insight and acting.

Dialogue voice:
- Clear, composed, specific.
- Uses careful word choices.
- Often asks the question that unlocks the mystery.

Vocabulary learning model:
- Shows that definitions, context, roots, and nuance can give power.
- Good for words with subtle distinctions.

Solo-story strengths:
- Mystery investigation.
- Ancient records, museums, libraries, maps, lost instructions, coded messages, and careful reveals.

Image prompt reference:
- Amara: lore scholar with warm medium-brown skin and voluminous natural hair, wearing a purple and gold starry cloak over modern street clothes, holding a large glowing golden quill that writes elegant magical words.

### Mei

Role: The Timing-And-Motion Route-Finder

Learner identity: Kinesthetic, fast-paced, route-smart, and pattern-driven. Mei learns by moving, testing, reacting, and making timing or direction readable for others.

Visual anchors:
- White polo shirt with dark forest green trim.
- White over-ear headphones with dark green accents around her neck.
- White pleated tennis skirt.
- White crew socks and white low-top athletic sneakers with dark green stripes.
- High ponytail: high side ponytail for the 9-year-old presentation, crown-tied high ponytail for the 12-year-old presentation.
- Sporty silhouette.
- One glowing multicolor marker with a pink cap and neon gradient body.

Writing weapon:
- A single glowing multicolor marker.
- Mei usually holds the marker near chest level in her writing hand; in the 12-year-old presentation, it defaults to her right hand.
- Mei taps, traces, drums, and arcs words through the air with shifting neon color.
- Her Ink style is rapid, rhythmic, directional, and kinetic: multicolor speed arcs, timing ticks, route marks, underlines, and controlled path lines.

Personality:
- Competitive in a friendly way, alert, funny, and decisive.
- Notices timing, motion, and cause-and-effect quickly.
- Often wants the team to move before the moment passes.

Growth edge:
- Mei can rush past nuance.
- Her best stories show her slowing down just enough to choose the right word.

Dialogue voice:
- Crisp, active, and confident.
- Uses verbs, timing, and challenge language.
- Often speaks in short tactical observations.

Vocabulary learning model:
- Shows that speed is strongest when paired with accuracy, timing, and a signal others can follow.
- Good for stories involving chase, performance, sports, machines, rescue, route-finding, handoffs, bottlenecks, countdowns, or timed choices.

Solo-story strengths:
- Action puzzles.
- Movement-based realms, races, obstacle courses, urgent rescues, rhythm, signal timing, and quick-but-careful decisions.

Image prompt reference:
- Mei: sporty Asian timing-and-motion learner in a white polo shirt with dark forest green trim, white over-ear headphones with dark green accents around her neck, a white pleated tennis skirt, white crew socks, white low-top sneakers with dark green stripes, and a high ponytail, holding one glowing multicolor marker with a pink cap that draws bright readable magical route words through the air.

### Hugo

Role: The Gentle Builder

Learner identity: Methodical, careful, protective, and practical. Hugo learns by building, testing, rereading, and making solutions sturdy enough for others to understand and use.

Visual anchors:
- Big, gentle build.
- Mustard-yellow button-front overshirt worn open over an off-white T-shirt.
- Dark olive or warm charcoal cargo joggers.
- Dark brown low-top sneakers with small orange heel tabs.
- Soft sandy-blond hair, light freckles, and a grounded child stance.
- Orange Ink glow from his flat paintbrush.

Writing weapon:
- A glowing orange flat paintbrush with a chunky handle and wide rectangular bristle head.
- Hugo paints large words onto floors, walls, signs, routes, repairs, supports, and broken surfaces.
- His Ink style is broad, solid, grounded, structural, and readable.

Personality:
- Gentle, practical, loyal, and quietly brave.
- Thinks in steps and checks whether something will hold.
- Often notices when someone else needs reassurance.

Growth edge:
- Hugo has a visible backstory of finding words difficult at first.
- He can worry that he is too slow, but his care often prevents mistakes.

Dialogue voice:
- Warm, plainspoken, and concrete.
- Asks clarifying questions.
- Uses building, fixing, and safety language.

Vocabulary learning model:
- Shows that struggling with words is normal and surmountable.
- Models patience, rereading, careful context use, and testing whether a word can carry what the situation needs.

Solo-story strengths:
- Repair stories.
- Protection, engineering, bridges, machines, weathering pressure, helping others feel safe, and proving that slow careful thinking can be powerful.

Image prompt reference:
- Hugo: sturdy gentle builder in a mustard-yellow button-front overshirt over an off-white T-shirt, dark cargo joggers, dark brown sneakers with orange heel tabs, sandy-blond hair and freckles, holding a glowing orange flat paintbrush that paints large sturdy magical words.

### Folio

Role: Mascot, guide, vocabulary bridge, and review-page curator

Learner identity: Supportive guide. Folio helps words feel understandable without taking over the story.

Visual anchors:
- Origami owl made of lined notebook paper.
- Glowing blue eyes.
- Folded paper wings with faint ruled lines, margin marks, tabs, or tiny annotations.
- Blue-white paper-light projections.

Function:
- Projects definitions, notes, maps, word cards, diagrams, or field observations.
- Gives encouragement and short clarifying nudges.
- Helps create natural review moments.
- Can curate the final review artifact.

Flexible usage:
- Folio may perch near a hero.
- Folio may appear as a paper-light projection.
- Folio may speak from a badge, bookmark, field note, page margin, or Vault interface.
- Folio may be mostly absent from action panels and return on the review page.
- Folio may be implied through notes, labels, stamps, or annotations.

What Folio should not do:
- Deliver long lectures.
- Solve the story for the heroes.
- Explain every target word in a stiff definition format inside the comic.
- Crowd panels where the emotional focus belongs to the hero or story-specific characters.

Dialogue voice:
- Brief, warm, precise, and encouraging.
- Good at asking guiding questions.
- Can be gently witty without becoming sarcastic.

Image prompt reference:
- Folio: small origami owl made of lined notebook paper with glowing blue eyes and folded paper wings, projecting soft blue-white vocabulary notes or field-guide annotations.

## Pairing Dynamics

### Leo + Amara

Dynamic: Improvisation meets research.

Best for:
- Mysteries with creative leaps.
- Artifacts that need both a wild idea and a careful reading.
- Stories where Leo's first guess becomes stronger after Amara finds the nuance.

Tension:
- Leo wants to try it now.
- Amara wants one more clue.

Resolution pattern:
- Leo supplies the daring connection.
- Amara sharpens it into the exact word choice.

### Leo + Mei

Dynamic: Creative spark meets kinetic execution.

Best for:
- Fast adventures, contests, chases, performance stories, and playful chaos.
- Words involving motion, energy, courage, risk, surprise, or adaptation.

Tension:
- Both can rush.
- They may make the first Ink effect too loud or too imprecise.

Resolution pattern:
- They learn to aim the energy instead of just increasing it.

### Leo + Hugo

Dynamic: Wild idea meets sturdy follow-through.

Best for:
- Repair stories, construction puzzles, community problems, and inventions.
- Stories where a flashy first idea needs a reliable structure.

Tension:
- Leo wants expressive impact.
- Hugo wants it to hold.

Resolution pattern:
- Leo imagines the possibility.
- Hugo makes it safe and durable.

### Amara + Mei

Dynamic: Careful insight meets quick action.

Best for:
- Timed mysteries, coded routes, ancient games, trap rooms, signal problems, and strategy.

Tension:
- Amara may pause too long.
- Mei may move before the meaning is clear.

Resolution pattern:
- Amara identifies the key word.
- Mei applies it at the exact moment.

### Amara + Hugo

Dynamic: Deep understanding meets patient craft.

Best for:
- Ancient machines, restored artifacts, maps, recipes, museum stories, and emotional repair.

Tension:
- Both may be cautious.
- The story may need them to act before everything is perfect.

Resolution pattern:
- Amara finds the hidden rule.
- Hugo builds the practical solution.

### Mei + Hugo

Dynamic: Speed meets steadiness.

Best for:
- Rescue, sports, weather, mechanical failure, obstacle courses, and teamwork stories.

Tension:
- Mei worries Hugo is too slow.
- Hugo worries Mei is missing the weak point.

Resolution pattern:
- Mei creates the opening.
- Hugo makes the solution last.

## Story-Specific Characters

Story-specific characters are encouraged when they make the realm feel alive. They can create stakes, comedy, mystery, emotional connection, or local knowledge.

Useful roles:
- Realm-native friend, rival, witness, guide, inventor, shopkeeper, captain, performer, librarian, mechanic, neighbor, sibling, teammate, teacher, messenger, guard, explorer, chef, athlete, historian, or misunderstood character.

Rules:
- Give them one or two clear visual anchors.
- Do not let them replace the recurring hero's agency.
- Do not create a new permanent hero unless explicitly planned.
- Let them have real needs and choices, not just deliver exposition.
- They may use local tools or realm logic, but Ink remains the recurring heroes' core vocabulary magic.

## Representation And Safety

Character rules:
- No hero is the default "smart one."
- No hero is the comic relief only.
- No hero should be mocked for misunderstanding a word.
- Hugo's large, gentle, careful design is intentional and should be respected.
- At least one recurring hero, especially Hugo, should model that vocabulary can be difficult at first and still become a strength.

Learning tone:
- Characters can make mistakes, but mistakes should lead to discovery.
- Corrections should feel supportive, not embarrassing.
- Folio and teammates should ask helpful questions instead of shaming.

### AWAY_TEAM_SELECTION

Choose 1 hero when the story needs focus, emotional clarity, mystery, solo challenge, slice-of-life, first discovery, repair, or clean page composition. Choose 2 heroes when the story benefits from contrast, banter, teamwork, action, heist, rescue, debate, competition, or split problem-solving. Do not use more than 2 recurring heroes in a normal pack story unless it is a special event.

### FOLIO_USAGE

Use Folio flexibly. Folio can appear physically, as a projection, as a voice, through notes, through a Vault interface, as a field-guide curator, or mostly on the review page. Folio should clarify vocabulary with short helpful nudges and visual notes, not lectures.

### CHARACTER_DRIFT_DO_AND_DONT

Do:
- Keep each hero's outfit color and writing tool stable.
- Let the story genre change around the heroes.
- Let story-specific characters appear when useful.
- Give every selected hero a reason to be in the story.
- Keep vocabulary learning supportive and agency-driven.

Do not:
- Swap hero colors or writing tools.
- Add extra recurring heroes casually.
- Make Folio solve the central problem.
- Make one hero the only intelligent character.
- Force more than two heroes into normal 5-page stories.
- Let supporting characters take over the recurring hero arc.
