# The Vault Image Sheet

Runtime use: image generation
Payload rule: load this sheet when generating any image that depicts The Vault interior, Vault-adjacent UI elements, or a panel where Vault architecture is visible in the background

## Critical Rules

- Max 4 modules per panel. One active story object per focal moment.
- 50–60% negative space in wide shots. The Vault breathes — clutter kills its identity.
- Heroes must read clearly against backgrounds. Check signature color contrast before finalizing.
- Dome silhouette visible in any shot wider than close-up. No flat ceilings, no hard corners.
- Portals start from a physical story object, not empty space.
- No dense fake text, no tiny unreadable labels, no decorative machinery clusters.

## Core Visual Identity

The Vault is a clean futuristic story archive outside normal space and time: a warm domed chamber with smooth modular archive walls, a central circular map/portal platform, thin glowing catalog lines, page-shaped screens, a cozy reading island, and story-bearing portal objects.

The strongest visual read should be "simple futuristic archive hub with warm reading energy," not "complex library full of tiny props."

## Spatial Blueprint

Architecture: a rounded dome interior with smooth curved walls rising into a visible domed ceiling. The dome has thin embedded catalog line patterns (warm-gold arcs and node points) that echo the floor lines below. Single continuous shell — no hard corners, no flat ceiling panels.

Proportions (intimate grand / human cathedral scale):

- Archive walls rise approximately 3.5× child height before curving into the dome.
- Central platform spans approximately 4–5 child-widths in diameter.
- Total dome height from floor to apex is approximately 5× child height.

Floor: circular warm off-white surface with radial and concentric warm-gold catalog lines emanating from the central platform. The light floor keeps the space grounded and safe. Lines are thin, embedded, and glow softly — not raised or mechanical.

Ceiling / dome: deep teal ceramic surface — dark like a night sky. Thin warm-gold catalog line patterns arc across the dome like constellation paths. Paper-light motes drift against the dark dome. The apex fades slightly darker at the top.

Entry: characters materialize on or near the central platform via a soft page-turn glow or warm light bloom. No visible door, hallway, or external architecture.

Atmosphere: faint luminous motes (like tiny glowing paper fragments) drift slowly near catalog lines and around the dome's upper curve. Sparse and warm — not dust, not snow, not dense fog.

## Modular Visual Kit

Zone-specific visual compositions belong in `vault-zones-image.md`; this sheet defines the shared Vault kit those zones reuse.

Core modules:

1. **Archive Wall Module:** Tall teal/off-white wall section with inset story tiles (rounded-rectangle bays, ~1.5× child height each), readable tabs at child eye level, and warm vertical light strips between bays. Tiles are flat with subtle depth — not protruding shelves.
2. **Map/Portal Platform:** Circular floor platform with a raised brass ring edge, smooth off-white surface, simple realm map shapes projected or inlaid, and one active story object at center. Ring glows warm-gold when active.
3. **Catalog Line System:** Thin warm-gold or soft teal lines embedded flush in floors, walls, and dome. Radial paths from platform, arcing up walls, tracing constellation patterns across the dome.
4. **Page Screen Module:** One or two large page-shaped screens (portrait orientation, rounded corners, ~2× child height) mounted on or floating near archive walls. Frosted off-white surface with soft backlight.
5. **Reading Island Module:** Child-scale modular bench or short rounded seat with teal/off-white Vault backing, one or two small movable cushions or block stools (muted red or quiet moss green), a compact brass-base lamp or page-light puck, and a low rounded table. Warm wood is only a restrained accent. Positioned away from the platform, near a quieter wall section.

Object budget: max 1 accent prop per panel (a returned artifact, a Folio perch, a tool swatch card). Avoid piles of books, gadgets, tubes, carts, ladders, floating shelves, or loose papers.

Story-bearing object variants (interchangeable on platform or wall): book, file folder, scroll, card, archive tile, map, screen panel, wall tile, or simple framed door shape. Each reads as a single clean object, not an ornate artifact.

## Materials, Palette & Lighting

Material hierarchy (with texture finish):

1. **Matte deep archive teal** — smooth ceramic with subtle vertical panel seams. Dome interior, wall structural bands, and platform edges.
2. **Warm off-white** — brushed-linen texture with faint weave. Floor surface, inset wall panel faces, page screens, and platform surface.
3. **Warm wood** — visible oiled grain used sparingly. Reading island bench edges, low table, and child-scale surfaces only; never dominant enough to make the nook feel like an adult lounge.
4. **Soft brass** — satin finish, not polished mirror. Thin trims, lamp bases, platform ring, tab frames, catalog line nodes.
5. **Frosted glass** — translucent pale blue, used sparingly as screen or platform insets.
6. **Paper and parchment** — visible fiber texture. Story objects, labels, review notes, Folio-adjacent materials only.

Core palette:

- Vault warm light: #FFDFA3

- Aged paper accent: #E9DDBD
- Frosted pale blue: #BFDDE2
- Soft library shadow: #27343A
- Reading cushion accent: #B94A48
- Quiet moss accent: #6E8F5F

Lighting:

- Warm reading lamps and platform-edge glow at human height.
- Soft vertical wall lights in archive wall module seams.
- Thin warm-gold catalog lines glowing at low intensity (brighter when active).
- Soft blue-white Folio projections only when Folio or Vault notes are active.
- Bright hero Ink colors only where word magic is active.
- Portal light may tint the platform or nearby wall tile, but not the whole Vault.
- Luminous motes provide ambient fill in the upper dome area.

## Scale & Composition

Composition rules:

- Use the central platform, floor catalog lines, and page screen orientation to guide the eye toward the active story object or portal.
- Use broad quiet background shapes (dome curve, empty wall face, open floor) so characters and vocabulary remain the focus.
- Keep portal shapes distinct from Folio projections and hero Ink.
- In crowded panels, simplify archive tiles and background lines before sacrificing hero silhouettes or target vocabulary.

Include at least one scale anchor per panel: bench (child knee level), platform edge (ankle to mid-shin), lamp (shoulder to head level), or low table (waist level).

Hero readability against Vault backgrounds:

- Leo's crimson: back with teal wall or off-white panel. Avoid red cushions or warm portal glow behind him.
- Amara's purple: back with off-white or warm-lit wall. Avoid dark teal shadows without rim light.
- Mei's white outfit: back with deep teal or use darker outline/rim light. Avoid pale screens or parchment behind her.
- Mei's multicolor Ink: avoid placing over busy catalog lines or multicolor map paths.
- Hugo's mustard/orange: back with cooler tones. Avoid dominant brass/amber props behind him.
- Folio's paper-white: back with teal or shadow. Avoid frosted glass or pale panels behind it.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:

1. Active story function: portal, map, field note, tool tune-up, review setup, or quiet return.
2. Hero silhouettes, signature colors, and writing tools.
3. Readable target vocabulary, labels, or definitions.
4. Vault anchors: archive wall, platform, catalog lines, page screen, reading island.
5. Human-scale furniture details.
6. Background dome pattern and distant tile texture.

Shot types:

- **Close-up:** Clean surface, readable label, warm light, one active story object. Dome out of frame or soft bokeh.
- **Mid-shot:** Heroes plus central platform, one archive wall section, one page screen. Dome visible as curved upper edge.
- **Wide-shot:** Dark teal dome above, light off-white floor below, teal-and-off-white walls between. Circular platform with glowing ring, radial gold catalog lines on light floor, luminous motes against dark dome. Heroes small but silhouette-readable. 50-60% negative space.

## Visual Constraints

Do not:

- Make The Vault look like a classroom, medieval castle library, ornate fantasy library, sterile sci-fi lab, spaceship bridge, or museum lobby.
- Use dark horror lighting, spell circles, runes, or generic fantasy magic.
- Let screens and holograms replace archive walls, story objects, or reading warmth.
- Let background detail obscure hero outfits, signature tools, Folio, or active Ink.
- Add real-world brand logos, school names, or copyrighted book covers.
