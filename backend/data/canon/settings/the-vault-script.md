# The Vault Script Sheet

Runtime use: script generation
Payload rule: load this sheet when writing any Lexi Legends script that opens in, passes through, or references The Vault, its systems, or its story-bearing objects

## Critical Rules

- The Vault never solves the Story Realm problem. Heroes choose — the Vault offers.
- The Vault never lectures. Show vocabulary through clues, labels, map changes, and story consequences — not definitions in dialogue.
- Portals start from physical story objects, not empty space.
- All Vault movement is slow: panels slide, lights fade, pages turn. No fast mechanical action.
- Do not turn The Vault into a classroom or test room. It is an adventure hub.

## Core Identity

The Vault is the recurring home base of Lexi Legends: a clean futuristic story archive outside normal space and time where words, maps, artifacts, and Story Realm access points are organized into a warm adventure hub. It is safe, curious, and iconic. It behaves like a calm responsive archive that invites exploration, not a machine that commands the heroes or a teacher that lectures them.

## Story Function

Use The Vault for:

- Home-base openings where heroes discover a new word pack, clue, or story object.
- Portal launches where a story-bearing object activates and opens toward a Story Realm.
- Word-pack discoveries where catalog lines glow, tiles slide forward, or a map updates.
- Tool tune-ups where heroes refill or calibrate their writing tools before a mission.
- Review-page setups where Folio curates discoveries into a field artifact.
- Quiet endings where heroes return, file what they learned, and rest.
- Brief reminders that Story Realms connect through a larger archive (a Vault stamp, map overlay, or glowing catalog path).

## Available Setting Elements

Detailed visual descriptions belong in `the-vault-image.md`; zone story behavior belongs in `vault-zones-script.md`; zone visual compositions belong in `vault-zones-image.md`.

1. **Archive Wall Module** — tall modular wall sections with inset story tiles and readable tabs. Narrative use: discovering new packs, finding clues, revealing hidden titles.
2. **Map/Portal Platform** — central circular platform with a glowing ring and realm map shapes. Narrative use: launching portals, showing route options, displaying active story objects.
3. **Catalog Line System** — thin warm-gold or soft teal lines in floors and walls. Narrative use: guiding attention, responding to correct word use, flickering on vague attempts.
4. **Page Screen Module** — large page-shaped screens. Narrative use: displaying information, showing realm previews, presenting review artifacts.
5. **Reading Island Module** — child-scale modular bench, small cushion, compact lamp or page-light, and low table. Narrative use: quiet moments, reflection, encouragement, session endings.

Story-bearing objects that can open portals: books, files, scrolls, cards, archive tiles, maps, screens, wall tiles, or doors.

## Behavior Rules

Allowed behavior:

- Catalog lines glow when a story object is relevant.
- One tile, book, file, scroll, card, map, or page screen may slide forward, open, unfold, or project a small clue.
- The central platform may show realm silhouettes, route lines, clue markers, or a changing story border.
- A tool-tuning surface may glow or display swatches.
- The reading island may warm, dim, or brighten for quiet endings.

Meaning response:

- Precise word: correct tile glows, map label clarifies, blurred title restores, safe portal cue opens, or hidden index line appears.
- Vague or incorrect word: catalog line flickers, splits, dims, or gently reroutes without punishment.
- The Vault invites another try or closer observation; it does not scold.

## Word, Ink & Language Behavior

Use readable English when a title, label, tab, map label, clue, definition, or target word matters. Important vocabulary must never be hidden inside ornamental script.

Ink remains writing-based: tags, cursive, multicolor marker route marks, paint bands, labels, notes, underlines, stamps, diagrams, and readable word forms.

The Vault can receive Ink on platform plates, page screens, tool swatch strips, note cards, archive tabs, or temporary paper-light overlays. Ink does not permanently mark walls or furniture; marks glow, settle, lift, or file themselves.

The Vault does not replace character-specific Ink styles. Leo's cyan graffiti, Amara's gold cursive, Mei's shifting neon multicolor route marks, Hugo's orange block lettering, and Folio's blue-white paper-light stay visually distinct.

## Hero Interaction

Tool behavior:

- The Vault may provide places to refill, tune, test, or file the heroes' writing tools.
- The Vault must not replace a hero's signature tool with a wand, tablet, scanner, staff, or weapon.

Agency preservation:

- The Vault can present options, hold clues, and organize discoveries.
- The Vault must not feel like adult authority judging the heroes.
- Heroes choose which portal to enter, which clue to follow, and which word to try.

## Variation & Constraints

Allowed variation: which module is active, portal color, local Story Realm props brought back, warm vs cool lighting emphasis, portal spill light, detail level, and whether Folio is present, implied, or absent. Do not treat variations as separate Vault versions — they are views of the same hub.

Narrative constraints:

- Do not require a Vault opening, Lexi Monster, or review page format in every story.
- Do not make The Vault feel like adult authority judging the heroes.
- Do not invent a replacement magic system unrelated to Ink and vocabulary meaning.
- Do not default to complex mechanical set pieces (rail carts, pneumatic tubes, elaborate lifts). If one appears, use it as a single story-specific accent.
