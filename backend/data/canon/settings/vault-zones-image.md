# Vault Zones Image Sheet

Runtime use: image generation
Payload rule: load this sheet when a named Vault zone appears visually: Reading Stacks, Map Platform, Ink Well, Field Desk, or Quiet Nook

## Critical Rules

- The zones are staging areas inside one Vault, not five separate settings.
- Preserve the Vault identity: rounded dome, teal/off-white modular archive walls, warm-gold catalog lines, central circular platform, page screens, reading warmth.
- Use one focal zone per panel unless the shot is a wide establishing view.
- Use 2 to 4 Vault modules per panel and one active story object per focal moment.
- Keep target words, clues, labels, and definitions readable.
- Keep hero silhouettes, signature colors, and writing tools clear before adding zone detail.
- Do not turn any zone into a classroom, ordinary library, museum display, spaceship console, fantasy workshop, or cluttered prop room.

## Core Visual Identity

Vault zones are recognizable arrangements of the same modular visual kit. The viewer should feel the scene has shifted function, not location.

Shared visual kit:

- Smooth teal/off-white archive wall modules.
- Thin warm-gold or soft teal catalog lines embedded flush in surfaces.
- Central circular map/portal platform with brass ring edge.
- Page-shaped screens with frosted off-white surfaces.
- A junior-scale reading nook kit: modular child bench, small movable cushions, compact lamp, low table, and teal/off-white Vault surfaces.
- Simple story-bearing objects such as books, files, scrolls, cards, tiles, maps, or framed door shapes.

The zones should look clean, reusable, and asset-ready. Use broad surfaces, repeated geometry, sparse props, and strong negative space.

## Spatial Blueprint

Default Vault zone relationship:

- Reading Stacks occupy archive wall modules around the room.
- Map Platform is the central floor platform.
- Ink Well is a small tool-tuning station attached to the platform edge or a nearby wall module.
- Field Desk is a clean review surface or page-screen workspace near the reading island.
- Quiet Nook is a warmer edge area with child-scale modular seating, small movable cushions, compact lamp, low table, and clear teal/off-white Vault backing.

Do not draw hard walls, corridors, doors, or different architecture between zones. They are parts of a continuous domed archive chamber.

Wide shots may show several zones, but the focal zone should dominate through light, character placement, and catalog-line direction.

## Zone Rules

### Reading Stacks

Visual function: discovery wall and story-object selection.

Required anchors:

- Tall teal/off-white archive wall section.
- Inset rounded-rectangle story tile bays.
- Child-eye-level tabs or labels.
- One active tile, file, book, card, or story object glowing softly.
- Warm vertical light strips between bays.

Composition:

- Use a hero or Folio at child scale near one active object.
- Keep distant tile rows simplified into clean shapes.
- Make one readable title, clue, missing word, or tab larger than surrounding marks.

Avoid:

- Dense shelf forests.
- Piles of books.
- Tiny fake text across every tile.
- Rolling ladders, carts, or old-library furniture.

### Map Platform

Visual function: central orientation and portal launch.

Required anchors:

- Circular floor platform with raised brass ring edge.
- Smooth warm off-white platform surface.
- Simple projected or inlaid realm map shapes.
- One active story-bearing object at the center.
- Radial warm-gold catalog lines leading to or from the platform.

Composition:

- Keep open floor space around the platform.
- Show portal light tinting the platform or nearby tile only, not the whole room.
- Make realm cues graphic and simple: silhouettes, route lines, border glow, clue markers.

Avoid:

- Calling it or drawing it as a table by default.
- Generic swirling portals detached from objects.
- Dense hologram dashboards.
- Complex mechanical lifts or control-room machinery.

### Ink Well

Visual function: small tool-tuning and Ink test station.

Required anchors:

- Compact station attached to the platform edge or wall module.
- Off-white test surface, swatch strip, shallow tray, or small page screen.
- Thin brass or teal frame.
- Small clean places for temporary Ink marks.
- Hero writing tool remains visually dominant over the station.

Composition:

- Show the hero testing a readable word, stroke, underline, label, or swatch.
- Keep Ink colors distinct from Vault lights: Leo cyan, Amara gold, Mei multicolor, Hugo orange, Folio blue-white.
- Use one test response at a time so the panel does not become a rainbow board.

Avoid:

- Cauldrons, potion bottles, fantasy fountains, weapon racks, upgrade stations, or lab machinery.
- Replacing the hero's signature tool with a Vault device.
- Dense UI meters, levels, grades, or scores.

### Field Desk

Visual function: evidence surface and review-artifact workspace.

Required anchors:

- Clean low desk, review surface, or page-screen workspace.
- Warm off-white working surface with teal or brass edge.
- A small number of notes, tabs, stamps, maps, cards, or visual callbacks.
- Folio projection or paper-light annotation when useful.
- Readable target word and definition pairs only when this is a review setup or review page.

Composition:

- Arrange objects in simple clusters with clear reading order.
- Leave enough blank surface around important words.
- Use one artifact logic per panel: case file, map, recipe card, exhibit label, route card, blueprint, or similar.

Avoid:

- School desks, worksheets, quizzes, lined notebook pages full of tiny writing, teacher red marks, or cluttered detective boards.
- Too many cards or callbacks for one panel.

### Quiet Nook

Visual function: warm reflection and ending space.

Required anchors:

- Child-scale modular bench or short rounded seat built into a teal/off-white Vault wall edge.
- One or two small movable cushions or block stools in muted red and quiet moss green.
- Compact brass-base lamp, page-light puck, or warm reading light at child height.
- Low rounded table, small side surface, or page-shaped note stand.
- Visible Vault continuity: catalog lines, off-white floor, teal backing, or a nearby page-shaped screen.
- Reduced active screens and calmer catalog-line glow.

Composition:

- Use warmer, lower light than action zones, but keep the surface treatment clean and futuristic.
- Give characters comfortable space and readable body language.
- Keep background archive shapes visible enough to remain The Vault.
- Keep wood as a small accent on the bench edge or tabletop; teal/off-white Vault materials should dominate.
- Add youthful readability through scale, rounded simple shapes, and small cushions, not toy clutter.

Avoid:

- Turning the nook into a living room, classroom reading corner, cafe, bedroom, adult lounge, club booth, spa alcove, or ordinary public-library alcove.
- Oversized curved sofas, high-backed upholstered booths, ornate wood paneling, decorative gold murals, potted plants, blankets, toy piles, or sentimental clutter.

## Materials, Palette & Lighting

Use The Vault palette consistently:

- Deep archive teal: #224E5A
- Warm off-white: #F3F0E4
- Warm-gold light: #FFDFA3
- Soft brass line: #C79B44
- Warm wood accent: #7A5634
- Aged paper accent: #E9DDBD
- Frosted pale blue: #BFDDE2
- Soft library shadow: #27343A
- Reading cushion red: #B94A48
- Quiet moss accent: #6E8F5F

Material hierarchy:

1. Matte teal ceramic for wall bands, dome, and structural edges.
2. Warm off-white brushed surfaces for floors, panels, screens, platform, and work areas.
3. Satin brass for rings, thin trims, lamp bases, and catalog nodes.
4. Warm wood only as a restrained accent on the reading island, desk, low table, and child-scale furniture; do not let it dominate the Quiet Nook.
5. Paper texture only on story objects, notes, labels, and Folio-adjacent materials.

Lighting:

- Reading Stacks: warm vertical wall lights plus one active tile glow.
- Map Platform: platform ring and radial catalog lines guide the eye.
- Ink Well: small focused light on the test surface.
- Field Desk: soft task light and readable paper-light notes.
- Quiet Nook: warm lamp light, lower contrast, fewer active screens.

## Scale & Composition

Scale anchors:

- Archive tile bay: about 1.5 child heights.
- Page screen: about 2 child heights.
- Platform edge: ankle to mid-shin height.
- Bench: child knee height.
- Low desk or table: waist height for a seated child or low standing work surface.

Composition rules:

- Use catalog lines, screen angles, and character gaze to point toward the active zone function.
- Simplify background tile patterns before sacrificing faces, tools, or readable words.
- Keep wide Vault zone shots breathable, with visible dome curve and strong negative space.
- Use close-ups for a single clue, test mark, story object, or review note.

## Hero Readability / Text Readability

Hero contrast:

- Leo's crimson should sit against teal or off-white, not red cushions or warm portal spill.
- Amara's purple needs off-white, warm light, or rim light against teal shadow.
- Mei's white outfit needs deep teal backing, outline, or rim light against pale panels.
- Mei's multicolor Ink should not overlap busy catalog lines or rainbow portal effects.
- Hugo's mustard/orange needs cooler backing, not brass-heavy clutter.
- Folio's paper-white body should sit against teal, shadow, or warm wood, not pale panels or frosted glass.

Text rules:

- Important words must be large, plain, and readable.
- Decorative background labels may simplify into clean shapes.
- Do not cover dialogue, target words, definitions, or story-critical signs with catalog lines, glow, glare, or hero Ink.

## Panel Priority

Preserve clarity in this order:

1. Active zone function: discovery, portal launch, tool test, evidence sorting, or reflection.
2. Hero silhouettes, faces, signature colors, and writing tools.
3. Readable target words, clues, labels, and definitions.
4. Zone anchors.
5. Shared Vault anchors.
6. Small furniture or prop details.
7. Distant archive texture.

## Visual Constraints

- Do not make the zones look like separate rooms with different architecture.
- Do not use hard corners, flat ceilings, normal doors, or external hallways.
- Do not use dense fake text, tiny unreadable labels, or decorative machinery clusters.
- Do not add school branding, classroom boards, test papers, grades, stickers, badges, or award displays.
- Do not use medieval fantasy furniture, runes, spell circles, cauldrons, or potion shelves.
- Do not let screens and holograms replace physical story objects, archive walls, paper texture, and warm reading energy.
- Do not make any zone visually busier than the main story action needs.
