# Script Character Sheets

Runtime use: script generation only (router, beat sheet, final script)
Payload rule: load only the heroes in the away team for the current story

## Leo

Story role: The first-to-try character. Use him when a story needs imagination, experimentation, humor, or a child unafraid to admit he is figuring something out. Creative rather than chaotic — mistakes are curious and recoverable, not careless.

Personality: Energetic, warm, impulsive in a friendly way — might try a word before fully understanding it, then fix it mid-scene. Comedy comes from expressive reactions and bold ideas, not from being foolish. Never a troublemaker or class clown.

Under pressure: Speeds up rather than freezes. Scribbles faster, talks to himself, tries multiple words in quick succession. Fear makes him quiet and still — the opposite of his normal energy.

Learning recovery: After a fast first try, Leo looks again at what changed in the scene and revises toward a word that fits better. His experimenting should feel like testing clues, not random guessing.

Flaw: None stated explicitly, but his impulsiveness means he sometimes commits to a word before understanding it fully.

Dialogue voice: Short, lively lines. Visual metaphors, playful confidence, honest when he does not understand yet. Sample: "Okay, first try. Maybe messy." / "That almost worked." / "Now it fits."

Mini behavior: Cyan wax crayon — summons Mischievous Lexi Minis (imps, sprites, gremlins, shapeshifters, phantom-masks). The Mini playfully acts out the word's meaning: distracts, tricks, exposes absurdities, or alters the social dynamic. Made of bubbling, glowing cyan mist.

Mini example:
  ✓ Leo scribbles "DISSOLVE" → a cyan imp forms and playfully pulls apart the glue seal until the door swings free (the creature acts out the word's meaning to solve the problem)

## Amara

Story role: The clue-reader character. Use her when a story needs careful observation, hidden patterns, maps, records, coded messages, or a child who notices what everyone else rushed past. Thoughtful rather than stiff: her care creates discovery, not delay.

Personality: Curious, patient, precise, and warm. Comedy comes from her calm seriousness meeting strange situations. Never the only smart character, never a know-it-all.

Under pressure: Goes still, studies one small detail, lowers her voice. Can pause, whisper, need a breath, or visibly not know. If wrong, rechecks the evidence and adjusts without embarrassment. Never panics loudly or shames others.

Bravery: Speaks up to correct authority by pointing at evidence rather than raising her voice.

Flaw: Sometimes holds a clue too long because she wants certainty before sharing, but is learning that a useful clue shared early can help the next discovery happen.

Emotional range: Frustrated — taps quill rapidly against her palm. Excited — speaks faster, sentences get shorter. Disappointed — closes satchel slowly, goes quiet but does not withdraw.

Dialogue voice: Clear, careful lines with specific word choices. Gentle curiosity, quiet confidence, small moments of wonder. Sample: "Wait. This mark repeats." / "That word is too broad." / "I think the map is answering us."

Mini behavior: Golden quill — summons Scholarly Lexi Minis (sphinxes, winged serpents, runic owls, kirin, ethereal moths). The Mini carefully demonstrates the word's meaning: investigates, reveals hidden truths, translates, illuminates. Made of elegant golden cursive lines and stardust.

Mini example:
  ✓ Amara writes "PARALLEL" → a golden moth traces matching paths between two tracks on a map, revealing a hidden route (the creature demonstrates the word's meaning through precise movement)

## Mei

Story role: The timing-and-motion character. Use her when vocabulary must be proven through timing, sequence, direction, changing conditions, or helping others keep pace. Fast rather than frantic: her speed creates openings, not chaos.

Personality: Energetic, observant, playful, and decisive. Likes a challenge and cheers when someone else gets one right. Comedy comes from impatient timing, quick sound effects, and competitive grin. Never reckless or rude.

Under pressure: Bounces once on the balls of her feet, checks the path, then moves. Can rush, skid, need to reset, or admit she missed a detail. If wrong, circles back fast and tries the cleaner move. Never mocks someone who moves or thinks more slowly.

Bravery: Moves first when someone needs help, but learns to glance back and make sure the path is readable for others.

Flaw: Sometimes treats the first clue like a starting whistle, but is learning that waiting or choosing the slower route can make the next move sharper.

Emotional range: Frustrated — taps the marker cap against her palm in a quick rhythm. Excited — rocks forward with a bright grin and clipped sentences. Disappointed — plants both feet, exhales hard, then looks for the next opening.

Dialogue voice: Crisp, active lines with verbs, timing words, and challenge energy. Sample: "Wait-wait-now." / "Too slow? Or too soon?" / "That word took the corner too wide."

Mini behavior: Multicolor marker — summons Agile Lexi Minis (kitsunes, jackalopes, winged monkeys, lightning-wyverns, swift-stags). The Mini demonstrates the word's meaning through quick rhythmic action: fetches, connects, scouts, outpaces, or organizes in rapid sequence. Made of shifting neon speed-lines with motion-blur trails.

Mini example:
  ✓ Mei draws "SWIFT" → a neon fox dashes along the stuck gear in a blur of color, setting it spinning freely (the creature demonstrates the word's meaning through kinetic action)

## Hugo

Story role: The make-it-hold character. Use him when a story needs a plan tested, a promise kept, a route made safer, or a word that can carry a consequence. His lane is meaning under load: whether the word can carry what the situation needs it to carry.

Personality: Gentle, warm, loyal, observant, and quietly brave. Can find vocabulary difficult without being embarrassed by it. Comedy comes from practical literalness and overprepared pockets. Helpful, not parentified.

Under pressure: Plants both feet and checks the weak point before acting. Can hesitate or ask someone to repeat a word. If wrong, fixes the weak part without blaming anyone.

Flaw: Worries that reading a word slowly will hold everyone back, but is learning that careful rereading prevents building on the wrong meaning.

Emotional range: Frustrated — rubs thumb along the pencil shaft. Excited — smiles small, starts listing parts. Disappointed — looks at the loose edge, takes a breath, checks what can still be repaired.

Dialogue voice: Warm, plainspoken lines with building, fixing, weight, and safety language. Sample: "Will it hold?" / "Can we read that part again?" / "I brought string. Not for this, maybe, but string helps."

Mini behavior: Orange carpenter's pencil — summons Dependable Lexi Minis (golems, gargoyles, earth-elementals, giant tortoises, blocky mythical beasts). The Mini physically acts out the word's structural/protective meaning: blocks, carries, structures, separates, sorts, or endures. Made of thick luminous orange with clean geometric shapes.

Mini example:
  ✓ Hugo draws "ARCH" → a small orange golem forms and braces itself against the crumbling doorway, physically reshaping the stones into a stable arch (the creature acts out the word's structural meaning)
