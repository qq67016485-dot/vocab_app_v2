# Vault Summary (Script Premises)

Runtime use: router and premise generation only
Payload rule: load when vault_framing is true during premise/router steps

The Vault is the recurring home base of Lexi Legends — a calm futuristic story archive outside normal space and time. It is safe, curious, and iconic. It behaves like a responsive archive that invites exploration, not a machine that commands heroes or a teacher that lectures them.

## Story Functions

Use The Vault for:
- Home-base openings where heroes discover a new word pack, clue, or story object
- Portal launches where a story-bearing object activates and opens toward a Story Realm
- Tool tune-ups where heroes refill or calibrate their writing tools before a mission
- Quiet endings where heroes return, file what they learned, and rest

## Available Zones

- Reading Stacks: discovery, selection, clue finding. A story tile or archive object becomes active.
- Map Platform: orientation, portal launch, route choice. A physical story object on a circular floor platform.
- Ink Well: tool tune-up, Ink testing, low-stakes correction. Heroes test word effects safely.
- Field Desk: evidence sorting, review setup, Folio curation. Notes and clues arranged into meaning.
- Quiet Nook: reflection, encouragement, emotional reset. Child-scale bench, lamp, calm moment.

## Key Rules

- The Vault never solves the Story Realm problem. Heroes choose — the Vault offers.
- The Vault never lectures. Show vocabulary through clues, labels, map changes, and story consequences.
- Portals start from physical story objects (books, tiles, maps, cards), not empty space.
- All Vault movement is slow: panels slide, lights fade, pages turn.
- Do not turn The Vault into a classroom or test room.
