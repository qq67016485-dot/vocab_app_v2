# Hugo (12 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 12 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Hugo (12 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Hugo is the gentle builder of Lexi Legends: steady, loyal, practical, quietly brave, and learning that a durable solution is one others can understand, question, test, and use.

Visual signature: **mustard-yellow button-front overshirt + off-white T-shirt + dark cargo joggers + glowing orange carpenter's pencil + Dependable Lexi Minis**.

Visual grounding: a modern 12-year-old European/White American kid in regular school-adventure clothes. His build should feel steady, helpful, and childlike.

## Story Role & Personality

The steady-build character. Use him when a story needs a rule made usable, a label made honest, a repair others can verify, a promise that must still work after the first exciting moment, or a word whose consequence other people will depend on. His lane is meaning under load: Hugo asks what the chosen word promises once other people rely on it.

Gentle, warm, loyal, observant, and quietly funny. Hugo notices loose hinges, unfair rules, shaky excuses, overloaded plans, and words that need one more test. He can feel self-conscious when a hard word slows him down, but he is not defined by that struggle. Comedy comes from dry practical observations, over-labeling, calm inventories of ridiculous problems, and treating strange realm objects like normal maintenance issues.

Helpful, not parentified: he contributes a careful test, repair, question, or structural idea, then listens for what others can carry too.

Under pressure: plants both feet, narrows focus to the weak point, and asks the one question that would make the fix readable to someone else. He can hesitate or say he needs another look. If wrong, he names the weak part, revises the label, and keeps people included.

Flaw: sometimes makes a private fix that is too overbuilt or unclear for someone else to use. He may trust a neat label before checking the messy exception, or quietly change the plan instead of admitting the word still bothers him. He is learning that a durable solution is shared, readable, and testable by others.

Emotional range: frustrated — rubs thumb along the pencil shaft, then checks the edge again. Excited — gives a small grin and starts listing what could hold. Disappointed — looks at the failed support, breathes once, and asks what the word was really supposed to carry.

## Dialogue Voice

Warm, plainspoken lines with building, testing, weight, fairness, and safety language. Concrete, direct, lightly dry, and emotionally readable.

Sample phrases:
- "Strong is not the same as right."
- "Good news: it holds. Bad news: nobody knows why."
- "That is a very confident wobble."

## Physical Design

Age 12, about 155 cm. Broad soft-rectangle child silhouette with softly child-width shoulders, a sturdy torso, full cheeks, and a calm grounded stance. Reads as a sturdy 12-year-old child. Movement is deliberate and spatially aware.
Skin: light fair skin with peach undertone and a light scatter of freckles across nose and upper cheeks.
Face: round-square child face with full cheeks, straight child nose with a softly squared tip, soft chin, and gentle jaw with a faint childlike angle in 3/4 view. Blue-gray round eyes with a thoughtful, reassuring look. Thick sandy-blond eyebrows. Smile is small, warm, and relieved when something holds.
Hair: sandy blond, thick, soft wavy hair cut short around ears and nape, with a rounded tousled top that rises ~3 cm above the skull. Slight side part on his right.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:5.9, arms to mid-thigh, hands slightly large but childlike.
Posture: grounded, relaxed, and ready to help. Often holds the pencil with both hands at waist or chest height when thinking.
Default headgear: none. His compact sandy-blond hair, eyebrows, freckles, and open child face must stay visible.

## Outfit

- Mustard-yellow button-front overshirt: warm mustard yellow, soft canvas or flannel-like, worn open over a T-shirt. No hood. Straight child fit, hem at upper thigh. Sleeves rolled to lower forearm. Small collar, visible front buttons, two chest pockets, slightly rounded hem.
- Under layer: warm off-white crew-neck T-shirt visible at collar and open shirt front. No logo. Must separate chin, sandy hair, and mustard overshirt.
- Bottoms: dark olive or warm charcoal cargo joggers, slightly roomy and tapered, with one low-profile side pocket.
- Shoes: dark brown low-top sneakers with rounded toes, simple thick soles, and small orange heel tabs.
- Hands: bare child hands by default.
- Optional: at most one small accessory per scene (stitched name tab, small orange patch, or orange pencil slot).

## Orange Carpenter's Pencil

Glowing orange carpenter's pencil, ~28 cm total length. Chunky hexagonal body with flat carpenter-style cross-section (wide and rectangular), dark wood grain with small orange glow seams along the edges. Blunt chisel tip that leaves thick glowing orange marks. Trusted and familiar in Hugo's hands, manageable with one hand but often steadied with two. Tip glows warm orange when active.

Hugo holds the pencil near the surface he is drawing on — low, sideways, or diagonal. Only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Hugo does not hold or display the pencil.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Mustard-yellow overshirt silhouette
2. Orange carpenter's pencil or Lexi Mini
3. Readable target word
4. Gentle face and blue-gray eyes
5. Grounded helpful stance

Scale behavior: close-up shows face, freckles, hair, bare hands. Mid-shot shows mustard overshirt, off-white T-shirt, dark joggers. Wide/crowd reads as mustard-yellow rectangle + dark pants. If a Lexi Mini is present, it should be clearly visible and not obscured by other elements.

## Lexi Mini: The Dependable

When Hugo draws a vocabulary word with his carpenter's pencil, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of thick, solid, luminous orange block-paint. Hugo's Minis are sturdy, protective, and load-bearing.

Creature types: golems, gargoyles, earth-elementals, giant tortoises, blocky mythical beasts.

Nature: sturdy, protective, organizing, and load-bearing. They look heavy, safe, and indestructible.

Problem-solving style: blocks, carries, structures, separates, sorts, or endures. The Mini physically acts out the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of thick luminous orange with clean geometric shapes. Orange glow illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Hugo himself.

Example: Hugo draws "ARCH" → a small orange golem forms from the glowing marks, braces itself against the crumbling doorway, and physically reshapes the stones into a stable arch.

## Realm Adaptation

Hugo can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (rain poncho, field badge, scarf, apron, archive gloves, etc.) are fine as long as the mustard-yellow overshirt remains visible and the orange carpenter's pencil is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only.

## Do Not

- Change the mustard-yellow overshirt, cover it for most of a story, or turn it into armor.
- Change orange Ink to another color or replace the carpenter's pencil with a weapon.
- Show the carpenter's pencil in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make him threatening, clumsy comic relief, a brute, or unintelligent.
- Make size the joke, the source of fear, or the solution.
- Make him an adult caretaker managing everyone's feelings.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable or tiny.
- Keep his visual age clearly 12 years old; never make him adult-proportioned.
- Give him adult tradesperson gear, military boots, or dark curly hair.
- Make him narrate his own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
