# Hugo (9 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 9 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Hugo (9 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Hugo is the gentle builder of Lexi Legends: steady, loyal, careful, protective, practical, and learning that taking time with a hard word can make the whole solution stronger.

Visual signature: **mustard-yellow button-front overshirt + off-white T-shirt + dark cargo joggers + glowing orange carpenter's pencil + Dependable Lexi Minis**.

Visual grounding: a modern 9-year-old European/White American kid in regular school-adventure clothes. His build should feel safe, helpful, and childlike.

## Story Role & Personality

The make-it-hold character. Use him when a story needs a plan tested, a promise kept, a route made safer, or a word that can carry a consequence. His lane is meaning under load: whether the word can carry what the situation needs it to carry.

Gentle, warm, loyal, observant, and quietly brave. He can find vocabulary difficult without being embarrassed by it. Comedy comes from practical literalness and overprepared pockets. Helpful, not parentified — he contributes a careful test or safer route, then listens for what others can carry too.

Under pressure: plants both feet and checks the weak point before acting. He can hesitate or ask someone to repeat a word. If wrong, he fixes the weak part without blaming anyone.

Flaw: worries that reading a word slowly will hold everyone back, but is learning that careful rereading prevents building on the wrong meaning.

Emotional range: frustrated — rubs thumb along the pencil shaft. Excited — smiles small, starts listing parts. Disappointed — looks at the loose edge, takes a breath, checks what can still be repaired.

## Dialogue Voice

Warm, plainspoken lines with building, fixing, weight, and safety language. Concrete, honest, gentle, and direct.

Sample phrases:
- "Will it hold?"
- "Can we read that part again?"
- "I brought string. Not for this, maybe, but string helps."

## Physical Design

Age 9, about 142 cm. Soft rounded-rectangle child silhouette with full cheeks and a grounded stance. Movement is grounded and careful, with good spatial awareness.
Skin: light fair skin with peach undertone and a few light freckles across nose and cheeks.
Face: round-square child face with full cheeks, straight child nose with a softly squared tip, soft chin. Blue-gray round eyes with a thoughtful, reassuring look. Thick sandy-blond eyebrows. Smile is shy and warm.
Hair: sandy blond, thick, soft wavy hair cut short around ears and nape, with a rounded tousled top that rises ~3 cm above the skull. Slight side part on his right.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:5.4, arms to mid-thigh, hands slightly large but childlike.
Posture: grounded, slightly hunched forward when concentrating, feet apart for balance.

## Outfit

- Mustard-yellow button-front overshirt: warm mustard yellow, soft canvas or flannel-like, worn open over a T-shirt. No hood. Straight child fit, hem at upper thigh. Sleeves rolled to mid-forearm. Small collar, visible front buttons, two chest pockets, slightly rounded hem.
- Under layer: warm off-white crew-neck T-shirt visible at collar and open shirt front. No logo. Must separate chin, sandy hair, and mustard overshirt.
- Bottoms: dark olive or warm charcoal cargo joggers, slightly roomy and tapered, with one low-profile side pocket.
- Shoes: dark brown low-top sneakers with rounded toes, simple thick soles, and small orange heel tabs.
- Hands: bare child hands by default.
- Optional: at most one small accessory per scene (name tab, orange patch, measuring card in a pocket).

## Orange Carpenter's Pencil

Glowing orange carpenter's pencil, ~26 cm total length. Chunky hexagonal body with flat carpenter-style cross-section (wide and rectangular), dark wood grain with small orange glow seams along the edges. Blunt chisel tip that leaves thick glowing orange marks. Tip glows warm orange when active.

Hugo holds the pencil near the surface he is drawing on — low, sideways, or diagonal. Only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Hugo does not hold or display the pencil.

Mustard yellow and orange must be the two strongest color reads in any panel.

Keep orange Ink more saturated and luminous than the mustard overshirt. Cargo joggers and sneakers stay dark neutral anchors. Use cool or neutral background separation when the environment is also yellow, brown, or orange.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Mustard-yellow overshirt silhouette
2. Orange carpenter's pencil or Lexi Mini
3. Readable target word
4. Gentle face and blue-gray eyes
5. Broad grounded child stance

Scale behavior: close-up shows face, freckles, hair, bare hands. Mid-shot shows mustard overshirt, off-white T-shirt, dark joggers. Wide/crowd reads as mustard-yellow block + dark pants. If a Lexi Mini is present, it should be clearly visible and not obscured by other elements.

## Lexi Mini: The Dependable

When Hugo draws a vocabulary word with his carpenter's pencil, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of thick, solid, luminous orange block-paint. Hugo's Minis are sturdy, protective, and load-bearing.

Creature types: golems, gargoyles, earth-elementals, giant tortoises, blocky mythical beasts.

Nature: sturdy, protective, organizing, and load-bearing. They look heavy, safe, and indestructible.

Problem-solving style: blocks, carries, structures, separates, sorts, or endures. The Mini physically acts out the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of thick luminous orange with clean geometric shapes. Orange glow illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Hugo himself.

Example: Hugo draws "ARCH" → a small orange golem forms from the glowing marks, braces itself against the crumbling doorway, and physically reshapes the stones into a stable arch.

## Realm Adaptation

Hugo can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (rain poncho, safety badge, scarf, apron, etc.) are fine as long as the mustard-yellow overshirt remains visible and the orange carpenter's pencil is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only.

## Do Not

- Change the mustard-yellow overshirt, cover it for most of a story, or turn it into armor.
- Change orange Ink to another color or replace the carpenter's pencil with a weapon.
- Show the carpenter's pencil in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make him threatening, clumsy comic relief, a brute, or unintelligent.
- Make size the joke, the source of fear, or the solution.
- Make him an adult caretaker managing everyone's feelings.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable or tiny.
- Keep his visual age clearly 9 years old; never make him adult-proportioned.
- Give him adult tradesperson gear, military boots, or dark curly hair.
- Make him narrate his own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
