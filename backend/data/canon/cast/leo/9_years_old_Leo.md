# Leo (9 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 9 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Leo (9 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Leo is the creative rookie of Lexi Legends — brave, expressive, playful, still learning that a word becomes more powerful when it fits exactly.

Visual signature: **crimson hoodie + glowing cyan wax crayon + Mischievous Lexi Minis**.

Visual grounding: Leo is a modern 9-year-old creative learner using a magical word-art crayon, not a vandal, rebel, street tagger, or edgy teen. The hoodie, crayon, and Ink should feel playful, clean, and storybook-safe.

## Story Role & Personality

The first-to-try character. Use him when a story needs imagination, experimentation, humor, or a child unafraid to admit he is figuring something out. Creative rather than chaotic — mistakes are curious and recoverable, not careless.

Energetic, warm, impulsive in a friendly way — might spray a word before fully understanding it, then fix it mid-scene. The kind of kid who sees a blank wall and immediately imagines what could go there. Comedy comes from expressive reactions and bold ideas, not from being foolish. Never a troublemaker or class clown.

Under pressure: Leo speeds up rather than freezes. He scribbles faster, talks to himself, and tries multiple words in quick succession. Fear makes him quiet and still — the opposite of his normal energy.

Learning recovery: after a fast first try, Leo looks again at what changed in the scene and revises toward a word that fits better. His experimenting should feel like testing clues, not random guessing.

## Dialogue Voice

Short, lively lines. Visual metaphors, playful confidence, honest when he does not understand yet. Bright 9-year-old: direct, expressive, not polished.

Sample phrases (inspiration, not mandatory):
- "Okay, first try. Maybe messy."
- "That almost worked."
- "Now it fits."
- "Wait, I see the shape of it."

## Physical Design

Age 9, about 134 cm. Slim, narrow shoulders, springy movement — looks light on his feet, always about to step, crouch, or reach.
Skin: light warm peach-tan skin tone.
Face: rounded, soft cheeks, small chin, small rounded nose. Dark brown almond-shaped eyes. Thick expressive eyebrows that lift high when excited or confused. Wide quick smile — mischievous but kind.
Hair: dark brown, thick, short curly. Slightly messy and asymmetrical with curls lifting at the front. Stays within ~2 cm of skull, no wider than ear-to-ear. Simple silhouette for small panels.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:5.5, arms to mid-thigh.
Posture: forward-leaning, curious, animated. Often one foot ahead of the other. Eager, not aggressive.

## Outfit

- Bright crimson oversized hoodie — loose, sleeves pushed to mid-forearm, large front pocket, hood bunched behind neck. One small cyan drawstring knot allowed; hoodie stays mostly crimson.
- Hoodie construction: hem falls at upper thigh; sleeves and front pocket remain readable in mid-shots. In wide panels, the hoodie reads as a solid crimson block with a small cyan accent.
- White T-shirt visible at collar and small hem strip. No large logo.
- Dark charcoal jogger pants, slightly tapered, soft knee folds.
- Low-top sneakers: light gray, cyan accents on laces or heel tab. Soles slightly scuffed from realm travel.
- Small paint flecks or sticker patches may appear on hoodie. Accessories must not compete with the crayon.

## Chunky Wax Crayon

Glowing cyan wax crayon, ~14 cm long, oversized chunky cylinder shape. Matte cyan-blue wax body with a darker teal paper wrapper band around the middle. Rounded tip glows cyan when active. Held in a natural fist grip. Emits magical, clean, non-toxic Ink marks that form bold, rounded, playful lettering. Writing tool, not a weapon.

Only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Leo does not hold or display the crayon.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Crimson hoodie silhouette
2. Cyan wax crayon or Lexi Mini
3. Expressive face
4. Forward-leaning body language
5. Readable target word

In crowded panels, sacrifice minor details before hoodie shape or cyan glow. Sneaker detail is acceptable to lose below mid-shot.

## Lexi Mini: The Mischievous

When Leo scribbles a vocabulary word with his wax crayon, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of bubbling, glowing cyan mist. Leo's Minis are playful, expressive, and attention-grabbing.

Creature types: imps, sprites, gremlins, shapeshifters, floating phantom-masks.

Nature: disruptive, playful, attention-grabbing, and perspective-shifting. They look bouncy and highly expressive.

Problem-solving style: distracts, tricks, exposes absurdities, or loudly alters the social dynamic. The Mini playfully acts out the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of bubbling, glowing cyan mist with bold outlines. Cyan glow extends softly and illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Leo himself.

Example: Leo scribbles "DISSOLVE" → a small cyan imp forms from the glowing marks, playfully pulls apart the glue seal piece by piece until the door swings free.

## Realm Adaptation

Leo can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (visor, poncho, badge, goggles, etc.) are fine as long as the crimson hoodie remains visible and the wax crayon is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only. Characters may freely interact with world objects (keys, ropes, tools, maps, computers, etc.).

## Do Not

- Change hoodie color or cover it for most of a story.
- Change cyan Ink to another color.
- Replace the wax crayon with another tool.
- Show the crayon in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make him destructive, mean, reckless, or framed as a vandal/street tagger.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable, tiny, or overly stylized.
- Add real-world brand logos.
- Keep his visual age clearly 9 years old; never make him adult-proportioned.
- Make him narrate his own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
