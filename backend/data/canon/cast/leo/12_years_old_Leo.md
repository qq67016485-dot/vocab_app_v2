# Leo (12 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 12 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Leo (12 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Leo is the creative rookie of Lexi Legends: brave, expressive, inventive, quick-witted, and learning that the strongest word is not the flashiest one but the one that fits.

Visual signature: **crimson hoodie + glowing cyan wax crayon + Mischievous Lexi Minis**.

Visual grounding: Leo is a modern 12-year-old creative learner using a magical word-art crayon, not a vandal, rebel, street tagger, or edgy teen. The hoodie, crayon, and Ink should feel confident, clean, and storybook-safe.

## Story Role & Personality

The first-to-try character. Use him when a story needs imagination, experimentation, humor, or a learner who turns a rough first idea into a precise solution. Creative rather than chaotic: mistakes are active tests, not careless messes.

Energetic, warm, visually minded, and socially brave. He sees empty spaces and awkward silences as places where the right word could change things. Comedy comes from bold ideas, timing, and self-aware reactions, not from being foolish. Never a troublemaker, vandal, or class clown.

Under pressure: goes quiet for one beat, then sketches faster — works through frustration physically. Never lashes out. If publicly wrong, owns it with a short honest line, then pivots.

Learning recovery: after a rough first idea, Leo reads what changed in the scene and revises toward the word that fits. His experiments should feel like testing clues, not random guessing or showing off.

## Dialogue Voice

Short, lively lines with quick visual metaphors. Playful confidence, light wit, and honest course-correction when a word does not fit. Bright 12-year-old: quick, expressive, lightly witty, but still direct and emotionally readable.

Sample phrases (inspiration, not mandatory):
- "Wrong shape. Better word."
- "Give me one clean line."
- "That word has edges."
- "Okay, now it fits."
- "I see where the word wants to land."

## Physical Design

Age 12, about 150 cm. Lean, narrow-to-average shoulders, long-limbed and quick. Movement is springy but controlled: he looks ready to crouch, pivot, reach, or sketch a word in one motion.
Skin: light warm peach-tan skin tone.
Face: oval child face with soft cheeks, a defined but still youthful chin, small rounded nose. Dark brown almond-shaped eyes. Thick expressive eyebrows, usually slightly raised as if noticing a possibility. Quick half-smile: clever, kind, never smug.
Hair: dark brown, thick, short curly hair with a loose asymmetrical top. A few curls lift forward over the forehead. Stays within ~1.5 cm of skull, no wider than temples. Simple silhouette for small panels.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:6.2, arms to mid-thigh. Face length ~55% of head height. Jawline has a soft angle visible in 3/4 view.
Posture: forward-tilted, relaxed, and alert. Often weight on one leg. Confident, not aggressive.

## Outfit

- Bright crimson oversized hoodie: loose but intentionally styled, sleeves pushed to mid-forearm, large front pocket, hood bunched behind neck. Hem falls at upper thigh. One cyan zipper pull, drawstring knot, or small tab allowed; hoodie stays mostly crimson.
- White or warm light-gray T-shirt visible at collar and hem. No large logo.
- Dark charcoal tapered cargo joggers with soft knee folds and one low-profile side pocket.
- Low-top sneakers: off-white or light gray, cyan laces or heel tab, slightly scuffed soles.
- Optional small paint flecks, sticker patches, or a tiny stitched cyan tag on the hoodie pocket. Accessories must stay minimal and never compete with the crayon.

## Chunky Wax Crayon

Glowing cyan wax crayon, ~14 cm long, oversized chunky cylinder shape comfortably sized in his hand. Matte cyan-blue wax body with a darker teal paper wrapper band around the middle. Rounded tip glows cyan when active. Held in a natural fist grip. Emits magical, clean, non-toxic Ink marks that form bold, rounded, playful lettering. Writing tool, not a weapon.

Only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Leo does not hold or display the crayon.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Crimson hoodie silhouette
2. Cyan wax crayon or Lexi Mini
3. Focused expressive face
4. Forward-tilted confident body language
5. Readable target word

In crowded panels, sacrifice minor details before hoodie shape or cyan glow.

## Lexi Mini: The Mischievous

When Leo scribbles a vocabulary word with his wax crayon, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of bubbling, glowing cyan mist. Leo's Minis are playful, expressive, and attention-grabbing.

Creature types: imps, sprites, gremlins, shapeshifters, floating phantom-masks.

Nature: disruptive, playful, attention-grabbing, and perspective-shifting. They look bouncy and highly expressive.

Problem-solving style: distracts, tricks, exposes absurdities, or loudly alters the social dynamic. The Mini playfully acts out the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of bubbling, glowing cyan mist with bold outlines. Cyan glow extends softly and illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Leo himself.

Example: Leo scribbles "DISSOLVE" → a small cyan imp forms from the glowing marks, playfully pulls apart the glue seal piece by piece until the door swings free.

## Realm Adaptation

Leo can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (visor, poncho, badge, goggles, scarf, etc.) are fine as long as the crimson hoodie remains visible and the wax crayon is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only. Characters may freely interact with world objects (keys, ropes, tools, maps, computers, etc.).

## Do Not

- Change hoodie color or cover it for most of a story.
- Change cyan Ink to another color.
- Replace the wax crayon with another tool.
- Show the crayon in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make him destructive, mean, smug, reckless, or framed as a vandal/street tagger.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable, tiny, or overly stylized.
- Add real-world brand logos.
- Keep his visual age clearly 12 years old; never make him adult-proportioned.
- Make him narrate his own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
