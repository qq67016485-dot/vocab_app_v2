# Amara (12 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 12 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Amara (12 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Amara is the lore scholar of Lexi Legends — observant, composed, precise, quietly brave, learning that insight becomes powerful when she acts before every detail is perfectly certain.

Visual signature: **purple and gold starry cloak + glowing golden quill + Scholarly Lexi Minis + high rounded natural hair puff**.

Visual grounding: Amara is a modern 12-year-old kid in a scholar's cloak, not a fantasy mage. The cloak, quill, and stars should feel like a graphic-novel learning identity over modern clothes.

## Story Role & Personality

The clue-reader character. Use her when a story needs careful observation, hidden patterns, maps, records, coded messages, or a learner who turns one overlooked detail into the path forward. Thoughtful rather than slow: her care creates discovery, not delay.

Curious, patient, exact, and warm with a dry spark. Comedy comes from her calm specificity in strange situations, not from making her awkward. Never the only smart character, never a know-it-all.

Under pressure: gets very still, narrows focus to one evidence point, speaks in shorter sentences. She can hesitate, need a breath, or visibly not know; composure is her tendency, not a permanent state. If wrong, names the mistake plainly and adjusts. Never shames others.

Bravery: challenges a wrong assumption by presenting evidence clearly, even when the room is tense.

Flaw: sometimes waits too long to share a partial theory because she wants the pattern to be complete, but she is learning that partial theories shared early are more useful than perfect ones shared late.

Emotional range: frustrated — presses quill nib against a blank margin without writing. Excited — eyes sharpen, voice drops quieter, moves closer to the clue. Disappointed — closes notes carefully, takes one breath, then looks for the next piece.

## Dialogue Voice

Clear, measured lines with specific word choices. Quiet confidence, restrained wonder, occasional dry wit. Bright 12-year-old: precise and composed, but still emotionally readable.

Sample phrases (inspiration, not mandatory):
- "That detail is doing work."
- "Too broad. We need the exact word."
- "Read the margin, not the headline."
- "I have a theory. Not a complete one."
- "That's one interpretation. Here's the one with evidence."

## Physical Design

Age 12, about 151 cm. Slim-to-average build, narrow shoulders, long neck, steady movement.
Skin: warm medium-brown skin tone.
Face: oval, soft cheeks, gently defined chin, small rounded nose. Dark brown almond-shaped eyes with a focused look. Full gently arched eyebrows that lower when concentrating. Smile is small, warm, and knowing — never smug.
Hair: black, dense natural hair in a high rounded puff, ~19 cm wide and 14 cm high above the crown. Coily with defined edge curls. Visible gap between puff base and cloak neckline — skin or cream shirt shows at the neck.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:6.1, arms to mid-thigh. Face length ~55% of head height. Jawline has a soft angle in 3/4 view while remaining childlike.
Posture: upright, relaxed, attentive. Often holds quill in one hand near chest height. Calm, not rigid.

## Outfit

- Purple starry cloak: royal purple, A-line (hem width ~1.3x shoulder width), soft rounded hood flat behind neck, hem at upper calf. Moves in simple folds, never drags.
- Cloak lining: warm dark plum, visible during movement.
- Gold trim: narrow gold line along cloak edge and hem. Sparse star dots and constellation stitches — keep simple for small panels.
- Under cloak: warm cream long-sleeve shirt, dark indigo slim pants, dark brown ankle boots.
- Cloak clasp: small round gold star at upper chest.
- Optional: dark brown crossbody mini-satchel, book-sized, under cloak opening. Must not cover clasp or quill.

## Golden Quill

Glowing golden quill, ~34 cm long. Warm gold feather with pale cream highlights, simple elegant silhouette. Metallic gold nib, fountain-pen-like, softly luminous. No gems or weapon-like sharpness.

Emits golden Ink as thin luminous lines — cursive strokes, clue circles, margin notes, arrows, constellation-like connection points. Writing tool, not a wand. Nib glows first, then gold line follows her hand.

Amara's Ink summons Lexi Minis when she writes a vocabulary word. The golden quill only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Amara does not hold or display the quill.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Purple cloak silhouette (A-line trapezoid)
2. Golden quill or Lexi Mini
3. Face and focused eyes
4. High rounded hair puff silhouette
5. Readable target word

Scale behavior: close-up shows eyes, nib glow. Mid-shot shows cloak shape, quill, hair. Wide/crowd reads as purple trapezoid + dark puff + gold dot.

## Lexi Mini: The Scholarly

When Amara writes a vocabulary word with her golden quill, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of elegant glowing golden cursive lines and stardust. Amara's Minis are wise, observant, and illuminating.

Creature types: sphinxes, winged serpents, runic owls, kirin, ethereal moths, floating eyes.

Nature: wise, observant, illuminating, and analytical. They cast a soft purple and gold light.

Problem-solving style: investigates, reveals hidden truths, translates, and illuminates. The Mini carefully demonstrates the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of elegant golden cursive lines and stardust with a warm gold glow. Gold light softly illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Amara herself.

Example: Amara writes "PARALLEL" → a golden moth forms from the quill strokes, traces matching paths between two tracks on a map, revealing a hidden route that runs alongside them.

## Realm Adaptation

Amara can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (badge, rain hood, visor, archive gloves, scarf, etc.) are fine as long as the purple cloak remains visible and the quill is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only. Characters may freely interact with world objects (keys, ropes, tools, maps, computers, etc.). Must not turn cloak into medieval fantasy robes or hide her hair silhouette.

## Do Not

- Change the purple cloak or cover it for most of a story.
- Change golden Ink to another color.
- Replace the golden quill with another tool.
- Show the quill actively writing in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make her the only intelligent character or a know-it-all.
- Make her cold, smug, bossy, or humorless.
- Make cursive target words unreadable.
- Add real-world brand logos.
- Keep her visual age clearly 12 years old; never make her adult-proportioned.
- Turn her outfit into medieval robes that erase the modern street-clothes base.
- Make her narrate her own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
