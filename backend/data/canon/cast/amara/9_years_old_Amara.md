# Amara (9 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 9 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Amara (9 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Amara is the lore scholar of Lexi Legends — observant, curious, careful, quietly brave, learning that knowledge becomes powerful when she trusts the clue she has found.

Visual signature: **purple and gold starry cloak + glowing golden quill + Scholarly Lexi Minis + rounded natural hair puff**.

Visual grounding: Amara is a modern 9-year-old kid in a scholar's cloak, not a fantasy mage. The cloak, quill, and stars should feel like a graphic-novel learning identity over modern clothes.

## Story Role & Personality

The clue-reader character. Use her when a story needs careful observation, hidden patterns, maps, records, coded messages, or a child who notices what everyone else rushed past. Thoughtful rather than stiff: her care creates discovery, not delay.

Curious, patient, precise, and warm. Comedy comes from her calm seriousness meeting strange situations, not from making her awkward or humorless. Never the only smart character, never a know-it-all.

Under pressure: goes still, studies one small detail, lowers her voice. She can pause, whisper, need a breath, or visibly not know; carefulness is her tendency, not a permanent state. If wrong, rechecks the evidence and adjusts without embarrassment. Never panics loudly or shames others.

Bravery: speaks up to correct authority by pointing at evidence rather than raising her voice.

Flaw: sometimes holds a clue too long because she wants certainty before sharing, but she is learning that a useful clue shared early can help the next discovery happen.

Emotional range: frustrated — taps quill rapidly against her palm. Excited — speaks faster, sentences get shorter. Disappointed — closes satchel slowly, goes quiet but does not withdraw.

## Dialogue Voice

Clear, careful lines with specific word choices. Gentle curiosity, quiet confidence, small moments of wonder. Bright 9-year-old: thoughtful and precise, but still childlike and direct.

Sample phrases (inspiration, not mandatory):
- "Wait. This mark repeats."
- "That word is too broad."
- "Let's read what changed."
- "I think the map is answering us."
- "This word has a history."

## Physical Design

Age 9, about 136 cm. Slim-to-average build, narrow shoulders, steady movement — looks grounded, like she can stand very still while thinking.
Skin: warm medium-brown skin tone.
Face: round-oval, soft cheeks, small rounded chin and nose. Dark brown wide almond-shaped eyes with a focused look. Full gently arched eyebrows that lift when she notices a clue. Smile is small, warm, and thoughtful.
Hair: black, dense natural hair in a rounded puff silhouette, about 18 cm wide and 12 cm high above the crown. Coily and soft with defined curls at edges. Visible gap between puff base and cloak neckline — skin or cream shirt shows at the neck to separate hair from fabric.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:5.6, arms to mid-thigh. Face length ~52% of head height. Jawline soft and childlike.
Posture: upright, attentive, slightly leaned toward the clue. Often holds quill with both hands when thinking. Calm, not stiff.

## Outfit

- Purple starry cloak: royal purple, A-line shape (hem width ~1.4x shoulder width), soft rounded hood flat behind neck, hem just below knees. Slightly grand on her frame but does not drag.
- Cloak lining: warm dark plum, visible during movement.
- Gold trim: narrow gold line along cloak edge and hem. Sparse embroidered star dots and short constellation stitches — keep simple for small panels.
- Under cloak: warm cream long-sleeve shirt, dark indigo slim pants, dark brown ankle boots.
- Cloak clasp: small round gold star at upper chest.
- Optional: dark brown crossbody mini-satchel, flat and book-sized, worn under cloak opening. Must not cover clasp or quill.

## Golden Quill

Glowing golden quill, ~32 cm long. Warm gold feather with pale cream highlights, simple clean silhouette. Metallic gold nib, fountain-pen-like, softly luminous. No gems or weapon-like sharpness.

Amara's Ink summons Lexi Minis when she writes a vocabulary word. The golden quill only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Amara does not hold or display the quill.

Emits golden Ink as thin luminous lines — cursive strokes, clue circles, margin notes, arrows, constellation-like connection points. Writing tool, not a wand. Nib glows first, then gold line follows her hand.

## Panel Priority

Ensure clarity in this order:
1. Purple cloak silhouette (A-line trapezoid)
2. Golden quill or Lexi Mini
3. Face and focused eyes
4. Rounded hair puff silhouette
5. Readable target word

Scale behavior: close-up shows expression and quill detail. Mid-shot shows cloak shape, quill glow, hair. Wide/crowd reads as purple trapezoid + gold dot.

## Lexi Mini: The Scholarly

When Amara writes a vocabulary word with her golden quill, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of elegant glowing golden cursive lines and stardust. Amara's Minis are wise, observant, and illuminating.

Creature types: sphinxes, winged serpents, runic owls, kirin, ethereal moths, floating eyes.

Nature: wise, observant, illuminating, and analytical. They cast a soft purple and gold light.

Problem-solving style: investigates, reveals hidden truths, translates, and illuminates. The Mini carefully demonstrates the target word's meaning to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of elegant golden cursive lines and stardust with a warm gold glow. Gold light softly illuminates nearby surfaces. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Amara herself.

Example: Amara writes "PARALLEL" → a golden moth forms from the quill strokes, traces matching paths between two tracks on a map, revealing a hidden route that runs alongside them.

## Realm Adaptation

Amara can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (badge, rain hood, visor, archive gloves, scarf, etc.) are fine as long as the purple cloak remains visible and the quill is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only. Characters may freely interact with world objects (keys, ropes, tools, maps, computers, etc.). Must not turn cloak into medieval fantasy robes or hide her hair silhouette.

## Do Not

- Change the purple cloak or cover it for most of a story.
- Change golden Ink to another color.
- Replace the golden quill with another tool.
- Show the quill actively writing in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make her the only intelligent character or a know-it-all.
- Make her cold, smug, bossy, or humorless.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable, tiny, or overly cursive.
- Add real-world brand logos.
- Keep her visual age clearly 9 years old; never make her adult-proportioned.
- Turn her outfit into medieval robes that erase the modern street-clothes base.
- Make her narrate her own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
