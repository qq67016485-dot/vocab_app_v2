# Mei (12 years old) Visual Sheet

Runtime use: script generation and image generation
Age: 12 years old
Payload rule: load this entire sheet when Mei (12 years old) appears in a story

## Core Identity

Mei is the relay-minded route-finder of Lexi Legends: fast in reactions, sharp with sequence and direction, competitive in a friendly way, learning that the smartest route is the one everyone can read, trust, and follow.

Visual signature: **white polo with dark green trim + white pleated tennis skirt + headphones around neck + glowing multicolor marker + high ponytail + Agile Lexi Minis**.

Visual grounding: Mei is a modern 12-year-old sporty Asian learner using a magical multicolor marker for word-art, not a superhero, ninja, racer mascot, or sci-fi soldier. The polo, skirt, and marker should feel like refined graphic-novel learning gear over everyday athletic clothes.

## Story Role & Personality

The timing-and-motion character. Use her when vocabulary must coordinate moving parts: handoffs, sequence, changing routes, bottlenecks, pace mismatches, countdowns, or a plan that only works if every person involved gets a readable signal. Fast rather than frantic: her speed creates openings, not chaos.

Alert, decisive, funny, and aware of the people around her. She notices handoffs, bottlenecks, and who might get left behind by a route. She respects the challenge more than the win. Comedy comes from quick route comments, over-serious countdowns for tiny tasks, and inventing route names she regrets mid-run.

Under pressure: goes still for half a beat, scans the route, then moves with purpose. She can tense up, miss a cue, or admit she assumed everyone saw the opening; confidence is her tendency, not a permanent state. If wrong, points to the bottleneck, asks who has the next count, and adjusts. Never mocks someone who moves or thinks more slowly.

Bravery: moves first when someone needs help, but checks whether her move creates a path for others.

Flaw: sometimes solves the route in her head before checking what everyone else can see. She is learning that leadership is not calling every beat herself; sometimes the strongest move is handing the signal to someone else.

Emotional range: frustrated — taps the marker cap once, then plants both feet. Excited — voice drops into a focused grin, shoulders angle toward the route. Disappointed — exhales through her nose, checks the path again, looks for the next cue.

## Dialogue Voice

Crisp, active lines with timing, direction, and challenge language. Bright 12-year-old: quick, lightly witty, route-smart, but still direct and emotionally readable.

Sample phrases (inspiration, not mandatory):
- "I saw the opening, not the landing. My bad."
- "Can someone else call the count? I'm doing the impossible part."
- "The route works. My signal didn't."
- "Tiny countdown. Very serious."
- "Wait. Who can actually see my mark?"

## Physical Design

Age 12, about 152 cm. Slender youthful athletic build, with soft energetic proportions. Movements are lively and light — can stop, pivot, sprint, or balance on a narrow path without wasted motion.
Skin: fair with warm peachy undertones.
Face: soft round-oval shape, full rosy cheeks, gentle rounded chin, small delicate nose. Large warm brown round eyes with bright sparkling highlights, showing a friendly and enthusiastic expression. Soft arched dark brown eyebrows. Bright wide smile that shows upper teeth, warm and genuine.
Hair: dark brown (near-black), thick and slightly wavy with natural soft flyaways. Styled in a single high ponytail tied at the crown with a thin red elastic hair tie, falling to the mid-back with bouncy flowing layers. Wispy side-swept bangs frame the forehead, ending just above the eyebrows, with a few loose strands framing the face.
Proportions: head-to-body ratio ~1:6.5, retains youthful proportions with soft unsharpened facial features. No makeup, no sharp adult jawline.
Posture: relaxed and upright, natural confident stance. Holds the glowing multicolor marker in her right hand at chest level when facing forward; arms hang naturally at sides when not holding objects.

## Outfit

- Polo shirt: white short-sleeved polo shirt with dark forest green trim. Contrasting dark green ribbed collar with a single white button placket at the neck. Dark green cuffs on the sleeves, with two parallel dark green stripes running down the outer side of each sleeve.
- Skirt: white A-line pleated tennis skirt, slightly above knee length, with a subtle dark green accent stripe on the left side (visible in profile view). Crisp, evenly spaced pleats.
- Socks: plain white crew socks, reaching mid-calf.
- Shoes: white low-top athletic sneakers with dark green stripes on the outer sides. Classic clean tennis shoe design with white laces and white rubber soles.
- Headphones: white over-ear headphones with dark green accents on the ear cups. Default position is around her neck. May be worn over ears when listening or concentrating.
- No excessive accessories; preserve the clean sporty silhouette.

## Glowing Multicolor Marker

One glowing multicolor marker, ~14 cm long, chunky school-marker shape with a rounded pink cap and blunt felt tip. Body features a bright neon gradient (yellow, green, pink, purple). Tip glows with shifting neon colors when writing. Emits clean, non-toxic Ink light that draws readable words, arrows, beats, and motion paths. Writing tool, not a weapon. Default: held in right hand at chest level.

Mei's Ink summons Lexi Minis when she draws a vocabulary word. The multicolor marker only appears on-page during a Lexi Mini summon (0-2 per story). In panels without a Mini summon, Mei does not hold or display the marker.

## Lexi Mini: The Agile

When Mei draws a vocabulary word with her multicolor marker, it hatches into a Lexi Mini — a temporary magical creature made of shifting neon speed-lines with motion-blur trails. Mei's Minis are kinetic, energetic, and fast.

Creature types: kitsunes (foxes), jackalopes, winged monkeys, lightning-wyverns, swift-stags.

Nature: kinetic, routing, sequencing, and energetic. They vibrate with kinetic energy.

Problem-solving style: fetches, connects, scouts, outpaces, or physically organizes things in rapid sequence. The Mini demonstrates the target word's meaning through quick rhythmic action to solve the story problem, then dissolves back into Ink.

Visuals: made of shifting neon (yellow/green/pink/purple) speed-lines with motion-blur trails. Multicolor glow extends softly with short afterimages. The Mini must be clearly visible and distinct from Mei herself.

Example: Mei draws "SWIFT" → a neon fox forms from the marker arcs, dashes along the stuck gear in a blur of color, setting it spinning freely.

## Realm Adaptation

Mei can enter any Story Realm. Temporary accessories (rain cover, badge, thin scarf, light gloves, relay sash, etc.) are fine as long as the white polo with green trim remains visible and the glowing multicolor marker is not replaced. The signature tool is for Lexi Mini summons only. Characters may freely interact with world objects (keys, ropes, tools, maps, sports gear, etc.).

## Do Not

- Change the white polo + dark green trim or cover it for most of a story.
- Change the multicolor Ink to a single flat color.
- Replace the glowing multicolor marker with another tool.
- Show the marker actively writing in panels without a Lexi Mini summon.
- Make her a superhero, ninja, soldier, racer mascot, sci-fi combat character, fantasy scout, or wilderness ranger.
- Give her armor, combat blades, gun-like marker props, or a full-face tech HUD.
- Make her mean, dismissive, reckless, smug, or obsessed with winning.
- Make target vocabulary words unreadable, tiny, or overly stylized.
- Add real-world brand logos.
- Keep her visual age clearly 12 years old; never make her adult-proportioned.
- Make her narrate her own learning explicitly (e.g., "I learned that X means Y!").
