# Team Selector Summaries

Runtime use: team selection step only
Payload rule: load only the heroes and pairings that appear in team_options

## How Lexi Minis Work

Lexi Minis are temporary magical creatures summoned by writing a vocabulary word with an Ink tool. The Mini physically acts out the word's definition to solve a story problem, then dissolves back into Ink. Each hero has a unique Ink tool and creature class.

Most vocabulary words integrate through dialogue, narration, world logic, visual clues, or character action — without any Mini summon. Minis are special events (0-2 per story) reserved for moments where a creature physically demonstrating the definition genuinely serves the plot.

✓ Hugo draws "ARCH" with his carpenter's pencil → a small orange golem forms and braces the crumbling doorway into a stable arch (the creature acts out the word's structural meaning)
✓ Leo scribbles "DISSOLVE" with his wax crayon → a cyan imp playfully pulls apart the glue seal until the door swings free (the creature acts out the word's meaning)
✓ Amara writes "PARALLEL" with her golden quill → a golden moth traces matching paths between two tracks, revealing a hidden route (the creature demonstrates the word's meaning)
✓ Mei draws "SWIFT" with her multicolor marker → a neon fox dashes along the stuck gear, setting it spinning freely (the creature demonstrates the word's meaning)

## Hero Summaries

### Leo

The first-to-try character. Brave, expressive, playful. Uses a cyan wax crayon to summon Mischievous Lexi Minis (imps, sprites, gremlins). Best for stories needing imagination, experimentation, humor, or a child unafraid to admit he is figuring something out. Speeds up under pressure; mistakes are curious and recoverable.

### Amara

The clue-reader character. Observant, curious, careful, quietly brave. Uses a golden quill to summon Scholarly Lexi Minis (sphinxes, winged serpents, ethereal moths). Best for stories needing careful observation, hidden patterns, maps, coded messages, or noticing what others rushed past. Goes still under pressure and studies one small detail.

### Mei

The timing-and-motion character. Fast, alert, funny, competitive in a friendly way. Uses a multicolor marker to summon Agile Lexi Minis (kitsunes, jackalopes, lightning-wyverns). Best for stories where vocabulary must be proven through timing, sequence, direction, or changing conditions. Bounces and checks the path before moving under pressure.

### Hugo

The make-it-hold character. Steady, loyal, careful, protective, practical. Uses an orange carpenter's pencil to summon Dependable Lexi Minis (golems, gargoyles, earth-elementals). Best for stories needing a plan tested, a promise kept, a route made safer, or a word that must carry consequences. Plants both feet and checks the weak point under pressure.

## Pairing Dynamics

### Leo + Amara

Improvisation meets research. Best for mysteries with creative leaps, artifacts needing both a wild idea and a careful reading, stories where a bold first guess becomes stronger after finding the nuance. Tension: Leo wants to try it now, Amara wants one more clue. Resolution: Leo supplies the daring connection, Amara sharpens it into the exact word choice.

### Leo + Mei

Creative spark meets kinetic execution. Best for fast adventures, contests, chases, performance stories, playful chaos, and words involving motion, energy, courage, risk, surprise, or adaptation. Tension: both can rush and make the first Ink effect too loud or imprecise. Resolution: they learn to aim the energy instead of just increasing it.

### Leo + Hugo

Wild idea meets sturdy follow-through. Best for repair stories, construction puzzles, community problems, inventions, and stories where a flashy first idea needs reliable structure. Tension: Leo wants expressive impact, Hugo wants it to hold. Resolution: Leo imagines the possibility, Hugo makes it safe and durable.

### Amara + Mei

Careful insight meets quick action. Best for timed mysteries, coded routes, ancient games, trap rooms, signal problems, and strategy. Tension: Amara may pause too long, Mei may move before the meaning is clear. Resolution: Amara identifies the key word, Mei applies it at the exact moment.

### Amara + Hugo

Deep understanding meets patient craft. Best for ancient machines, restored artifacts, maps, recipes, museum stories, and emotional repair. Tension: both may be cautious and the story may need them to act before everything is perfect. Resolution: Amara finds the hidden rule, Hugo builds the practical solution.

### Mei + Hugo

Speed meets steadiness. Best for rescue, sports, weather, mechanical failure, obstacle courses, and teamwork stories. Tension: Mei worries Hugo is too slow, Hugo worries Mei is missing the weak point. Resolution: Mei creates the opening, Hugo makes the solution last.
