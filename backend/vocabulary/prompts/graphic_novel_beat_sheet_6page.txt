Arc guidance:
- Design a 6-page arc that fits the premise naturally.
- The extra page gives room for deeper character development and more gradual pacing.
- Draw the reader in on page 1, develop and deepen the situation through the middle pages, and land a satisfying ending on page 6.
- Every page should give the reader a reason to turn to the next one — curiosity, wonder, humor, or tension all work.
- Bridge time, location, or situation changes so the reader is not disoriented.
- When a page's setting_key differs from the previous page's and the new place is reached by a magical/instant transition (e.g. the Vault leaf-tile travel) or a jump to an unrelated place, note an arrival moment in why_this_page_matters so the script step can open that page by re-establishing where we are. Ordinary walkable moves (room to room) need no arrival beat.
- page_turn_question should hook the reader into wanting the next page. For Page 6 (the final page), use null — there is no next page.
- Match the arc shape to the narrative approach. Not every story needs escalating tension. Alternatives:
  - Setup → exploration → discovery → complication → shift → reflection (observational)
  - Cold open → breath → build → deepen → turn → release (action)
  - Situation → complication → attempt → twist → surprise → resolution (light conflict)
  - Arrival → wonder → deeper look → challenge → connection → departure (slice-of-life)

Emotional pacing:
- At least 3 of the 6 pages should contain a register shift within the page (e.g., a quiet or humorous panel within an otherwise tense page, or a moment of wonder inside an action sequence).
- Assign a pacing_note to each page: "breath" | "build" | "turn" | "release" | "launch" | "deepen".
- Connect pacing_note to panel count:
  - "breath" → 1-2 panels (give art room to breathe)
  - "build" → 3-4 panels (progressive momentum)
  - "deepen" → 2-3 panels (character or world development)
  - "turn" → 2-3 panels (dramatic shift needs space)
  - "release" → 2-3 panels (resolution landing)
  - "launch" → 2-3 panels (dynamic energy)
