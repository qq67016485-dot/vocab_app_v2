# Lexi Legends Visual Production Bible

Status: Phase 1-2 production companion
Canonical sources: `lexi-legends-setting-bible.md`, `lexi-legends-cast-bible.md`, age-specific character sheets under `runtime_canon/lexi_legends/cast/`, and setting image sheets under `runtime_canon/lexi_legends/settings/`
Created: 2026-05-20

## Purpose

This production bible turns the Lexi Legends canon into practical visual direction. It is meant for image prompts, human illustrators, style frames, canonical character sheets, and later asset commissioning.

The goal is visual continuity without locking every story into the same look. The heroes should remain instantly recognizable across Story Realms, age bands, and genres.

For exact per-character prompts, load the matching individual character sheet for the selected age presentation. This bible gives compressed production guidance; the individual sheets carry the precise current outfit, tool, palette, body proportions, and "Do Not" rules.

## Core Visual Principles

Lexi Legends should look like a premium animated graphic novel for upper-elementary readers:
- Clean, readable comic panels with strong silhouettes.
- Expressive faces and body language.
- Bright magical Ink effects that are always writing-based.
- Cinematic lighting, but not dark or hard to read.
- Detailed enough to feel rich, simple enough for clear 5-page storytelling.
- Vivid colors balanced by warm neutrals, not a single-hue palette.

Do not make the IP look like:
- A textbook worksheet.
- Generic fantasy with random wands or spell circles.
- Dark horror.
- Flat stock illustration.
- A different art style on every page of the same pack.

## Age Presentation Presentations

Each generated pack should choose one Age Presentation presentation for the whole story: **9 years old** or **12 years old**. Do not mix 9 years old and 12 years old versions of the recurring cast in the same normal story.

These are not alternate-universe characters. They are production presentations of the same heroes for different learner age bands.

### 9 years old Presentation

Target reader: around age 9.

Visual read:
- Slightly younger proportions.
- Rounder face shapes and softer silhouette edges.
- Brighter, simpler outfit reads.
- Bigger expressions and clearer gestures.
- Less visual clutter on clothing and tools.
- More obvious emotional beats.

Story visual tone:
- Wonder, discovery, humor, friendship, repair, curiosity.
- Clear visual cause-and-effect.
- Fewer crowded panels.
- More direct camera angles and readable compositions.

### 12 years old Presentation

Target reader: around age 12.

Visual read:
- Slightly older proportions.
- Taller, more confident posture.
- More refined outfit layers and texture details.
- Subtler expressions.
- More cinematic camera angles.
- More controlled use of highlights, shadows, and environmental detail.

Story visual tone:
- Mystery, strategy, identity, social tension, layered choices.
- More nuanced facial acting.
- More complex settings, but still readable.
- Stronger use of framing, foreground/background storytelling, and visual clues.

## Character Visual Sheets

### Leo

Signature identity:
- Creative rookie.
- Crimson oversized hoodie.
- Cyan spray paint Ink.
- Energetic graffiti silhouette.

Core face and build:
- Lively, open face with expressive eyebrows.
- Lean, quick-moving build.
- Slightly messy hair, designed as a simple recognizable shape.
- Hands often active: pointing, sketching, tagging, holding the spray can.

Core outfit:
- Bright crimson oversized hoodie with rolled sleeves.
- Simple tee or layered undershirt visible at the collar or hem.
- Casual pants that allow motion.
- Sneakers with small cyan accents.
- Optional small sticker, patch, or paint mark detail, but never enough to muddy the silhouette.

Leo (9 years old):
- Visual age read around 9.
- Hoodie is a bit too large, making him look eager and scrappy.
- Rounder cheeks, bigger grin, more open gestures.
- Spray can looks slightly oversized in his hand.
- Expressions can be broad: amazed, sheepish, excited, determined.

Leo (12 years old):
- Visual age read around 12.
- Hoodie still oversized but styled with more intention.
- More confident stance, sharper grin, less flailing energy.
- Spray can handling is more controlled, like a practiced artist.
- May have refined street-art accents, such as a cyan zipper pull or small custom patch.

Ink VFX:
- Cyan paint mist, glowing graffiti letters, stickers of light, drips that turn into readable strokes.
- Words should look hand-tagged but legible.
- VFX for 9 years old can be bigger and splashier.
- VFX for 12 years old can use cleaner composition and intentional line rhythm.

Pose language:
- Leaning forward, crouched while tagging, one arm out for balance, wide-eyed discovery.
- Leo (12 years old) can use more relaxed confidence: angled stance, spray can low, eyes already solving the pattern.

Do not:
- Change the hoodie away from crimson.
- Give him a brush, wand, sword, or tablet as the primary tool.
- Make the graffiti unreadable when target words appear.
- Turn him into a reckless troublemaker. He is creative, not destructive.

Prompt capsule:
Leo, creative rookie, bright crimson oversized hoodie with rolled sleeves, casual sneakers with cyan accents, expressive lively face, holding a glowing cyan spray paint can that creates readable magical graffiti words.

## The Vault Visual Sheet

The Vault is the recurring home-base environment. For exact runtime use, load `runtime_canon/lexi_legends/settings/the-vault-image.md`; for named Vault zones, also load `runtime_canon/lexi_legends/settings/vault-zones-image.md`. This section is only the compressed production summary.

Overall read:
- Clean futuristic story archive outside normal space and time.
- Rounded dome interior with a deep teal night-sky ceiling above a warm off-white floor.
- Smooth teal/off-white modular archive walls with inset story tiles and warm vertical light strips.
- Central circular floor map/portal platform with a brass ring edge.
- Thin warm-gold or soft teal catalog lines embedded in floor, walls, and dome; dome arcs read like simple constellation paths.
- A few page-shaped screens, one cozy reading island, sparse paper-light motes, and story-bearing portal objects.

Core modules:
- **Archive Wall Module:** Tall teal/off-white wall sections with inset rounded story tiles and warm vertical light strips.
- **Map/Portal Platform:** Circular floor platform with a raised brass ring, simple realm map shapes, and one active story object at center.
- **Catalog Line System:** Thin warm-gold or soft teal lines that guide the eye without becoming busy circuitry; use radial floor paths and constellation-like dome arcs.
- **Page Screen Module:** One or two large page-shaped screens for maps, labels, review cues, or Folio-adjacent notes.
- **Reading Island Module:** Child-scale modular bench or short rounded seat, small movable cushion, compact lamp or page-light, and low table for warmth and quiet endings; teal/off-white Vault surfaces stay dominant over wood.

Materials:
- Matte deep archive teal, smooth warm off-white, soft brass, warm wood accents, sparse frosted pale blue glass, and paper/parchment only on story objects or notes.
- Avoid chrome, black glass, hard neon, dense machinery, medieval stone, decorative pipework, and ornate fantasy-library detail.

Lighting:
- Warm lamps and platform-edge glow at human height.
- Soft vertical wall lights in archive seams.
- Low-intensity embedded catalog lines, brighter only when active.
- Soft blue-white Folio projections only when Folio or Vault notes are active.
- Bright hero Ink colors only where magic is active.
- Portal light may tint the platform or nearby wall tile, but not the whole Vault.
- Sparse luminous paper motes may drift near catalog lines and the upper dome.

Object budget:
- Use 2 to 4 core modules in a normal panel.
- Use 1 active story object per focal moment.
- Use no more than 1 optional accent prop.
- Avoid piles of books, gadgets, tubes, carts, ladders, floating shelves, loose papers, tiny labels, and decorative machinery.
- Wide Vault shots need 50-60% negative space and a visible curved dome silhouette.

Recurring zones:
- **Reading Stacks:** Modular archive walls with inset story tiles.
- **Map Platform:** Central circular map/portal floor platform.
- **Ink Well:** Small tool-tuning station attached to the platform or wall.
- **Field Desk:** Clean review surface or page-screen workspace near the reading island.
- **Quiet Nook:** Youthful child-scale reading island with warmer light, fewer active screens, small cushions, and clear Vault teal/off-white backing.

Vault treatment (9 years old):
- Larger shapes, clearer icons, wider negative space, brighter wayfinding, and one obvious active object.

Vault treatment (12 years old):
- Slightly more layered archive tiles, subtler catalog lines, deeper wall scale, and more sophisticated map projections while staying readable.

Prompt capsule:
The Vault, a clean futuristic story archive outside time, rounded dome interior with deep teal night-sky ceiling and warm off-white floor, smooth teal/off-white modular archive walls with inset story tiles, central circular floor map/portal platform with brass ring, thin warm-gold catalog lines embedded in floor and dome like constellation paths, sparse paper-light motes, a few page-shaped screens, one cozy reading island, warm reading lights, and portals opening from physical story-bearing objects.

## Story Realm Visual Rules

Story Realms can vary widely by genre. The realm should carry the story's specific mood, but it should not erase Lexi Legends identity.

Realm flexibility:
- Pirate cove, cyberpunk market, moon station, science fair, ancient garden, subway mystery, bakery, sports arena, underwater city, museum, mountain observatory, or any other age-safe setting.
- Each realm may have its own palette, props, architecture, and local characters.

Continuity anchors:
- Recurring heroes keep their signature colors and tools.
- Ink effects stay writing-based.
- Vocabulary words remain legible when shown.
- Folio's paper-light notes stay blue-white when present.

Story-specific characters:
- Give each supporting character one or two readable visual anchors.
- Keep them secondary to the selected recurring heroes.
- Realm-native tools should not replace Ink as the heroes' core magic.

## Ink VFX Rules

Ink should always feel like language becoming visible action.

Allowed Ink forms:
- Handwriting, graffiti, annotations, labels, arrows, captions, diagrams, stamps, highlights, underlines, margin notes, paint bands, marker slashes, cursive loops, glowing word bridges.

Readability:
- Target words must remain readable.
- Avoid over-stylized lettering when the word appears in dialogue, captions, review artifacts, or educational context.
- If a word becomes a physical object, show a readable version nearby or integrated into the form.

Age Presentation differences:
- Ink for 9 years old: bolder shapes, clearer outlines, larger word forms, brighter glow.
- Ink for 12 years old: more controlled marks, layered meaning, finer linework, more cinematic integration.

## Shade Visual Rules

Shades are optional. Use them only when they fit the story.

Visual identity:
- Soft-edged shadows, torn ink, static, smudges, missing letters, flickering silhouettes, or page glitches.
- Charcoal base with violet or blue static highlights.
- They distort meaning rather than physically harm characters.

Age Presentation differences:
- Shades for 9 years old: softer, less frightening, more like smudges or mischievous static.
- Shades for 12 years old: more mysterious and visually tense, but still non-gory and age-safe.

Do not:
- Make Shades grotesque, bloody, or realistic horror.
- Give them weapons.
- Make their defeat violent.
- Use them in every story.

## Lexi Monster Visual Rules

Lexi Monsters are optional vocabulary manifestations. Use them only when the story naturally benefits from a creature or companion.

Design principles:
- The creature's shape, texture, behavior, and color accents should reflect the semantic content of the vocabulary words.
- Monsters should feel collectible and emotionally appealing, not threatening by default.
- A Lexi Monster should help, protect, reveal, carry, calm, repair, illuminate, or otherwise support the resolution.

Age Presentation differences:
- Monsters for 9 years old: clearer silhouettes, more charm, more obvious semantic features.
- Monsters for 12 years old: more elegant or unusual semantic combinations, richer textures, subtler symbolism.

Do not:
- Force a monster at the end of every story.
- Make all monsters look like battle pets.
- Let the monster solve the problem without the heroes understanding the words.

## Review Artifact Visual Rules

The review page is the sixth page and should look like an in-world artifact, not a textbook appendix.

Possible artifact forms:
- Vault Field Note, Lexi Codex page, map, case file, notebook, diagram, recipe, logbook, exhibit, message, field guide, machine panel, scrapbook, badge board, constellation chart, route card, poster, ticket, mural, blueprint, or any story-fitting object.

Rules:
- Every target word must be clear and paired with a short definition.
- Each word should include a visual callback to its story role.
- The artifact should inherit the realm's visual language while preserving readability.
- Folio may curate, stamp, annotate, project, or simply be implied.

9 years old review artifacts:
- Larger labels, clearer word-definition pairing, brighter visual callbacks.
- Fewer tiny details.

12 years old review artifacts:
- More sophisticated layout, layered clues, subtle annotations, richer object design.
- Still readable at graphic-novel page size.

## Production Acceptance Checklist

Before commissioning art or generating images, confirm:
- The pack chooses 9 years old or 12 years old and uses it consistently.
- Each selected recurring hero keeps signature color, tool, and Ink identity.
- Story-specific characters have simple visual anchors and do not displace the heroes.
- The Vault or realm has a clear visual mood.
- Ink effects are writing-based and readable.
- Shades and Lexi Monsters are only included when narratively appropriate.
- The review artifact fits the story and keeps all vocabulary readable.
